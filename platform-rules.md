# Platform Rules

## Reddit
- Read the subreddit rules before posting; do not infer from memory.
- If a subreddit bans individual/team help posts, frame content as general strategy, market behavior, or analysis — not as an advice request.
- Avoid “my team,” “your roster,” “should I trade,” “who wins,” player price checks, or league-specific details outside megathreads.
- Lead with a useful thesis, then explain the mechanism. No fake stats, fake tools, or bait tables.
- If AutoModerator removes a post, save the removal reason and rewrite to satisfy that exact rule before reposting.

## X / Twitter
- One clean idea per post.
- Prefer sharp mechanisms over vague motivation.
- No fabricated metrics, screenshots, model claims, or performance numbers.
- Avoid hashtags unless the account strategy explicitly calls for them.

## TikTok
- Keep videos under 60 seconds.
- Hook in the first 1–2 seconds.
- Use product-specific captions and templates.
- Do not rely on Google Drive assets until OAuth is verified; use local fallback when needed.
