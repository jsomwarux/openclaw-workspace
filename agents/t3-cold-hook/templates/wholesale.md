# Wholesale Distribution — T3 Cold Hook Template

## The Core Pain (for this niche)
Wholesale distributors with large catalogs (thousands of SKUs) burn rep time answering availability and pricing questions over the phone. After hours = voicemail. Multi-location = inconsistent answers. Manual reorder = stockouts. The catalog knowledge exists — it's just not surfaced automatically.

## Proof Point to Reference
"Built an automated parts lookup agent for H.C. Oswald Supply in the Bronx — surfaces 100 years of catalog knowledge through their existing chat channel in under 10 seconds, English and Spanish."

---

## Hook Signal Priority (use first available)
1. A specific review, article, or press mention about the company
2. A LinkedIn post by the key contact in the last 30 days
3. A job posting revealing a manual process pain (e.g., "must be able to answer product availability questions")
4. A specific company detail (# of SKUs, # of locations, # of years in business, a product category)

---

## Subject Line Examples (by hook type)
- Article/review hook: "The [specific detail from article]"
- Job posting hook: "The [role they're hiring] shouldn't need to know every SKU"
- Catalog/longevity hook: "[X] years of parts knowledge, one search"
- After-hours pain: "Parts lookup at 2AM"

---

## DM Template Structure

**Opener:** [Company] has [specific fact — # of SKUs, years in business, locations, or specific product category] — [observation about what that means operationally].

**Problem reframe:** Most of that institutional knowledge still lives in your reps' heads (or a filing cabinet), which means it disappears at 5PM and on weekends.

**Proof:** I built an automated catalog lookup for H.C. Oswald Supply in the Bronx — it surfaces their entire parts inventory through their chat channel in under 10 seconds, English and Spanish. Their reps stopped fielding basic availability calls.

**CTA:** Worth 15 minutes to see how it'd work for [Company]?
