# Property Management — T3 Cold Hook Template

## The Core Pain (for this niche)
NYC property managers with 30–200+ units spend hours per week on maintenance intake: classifying which vendor to call, confirming the tenant was notified, and following up when nothing happens. Most of this is manual — email chains, sticky notes, or a spreadsheet. The pain scales linearly with unit count.

## Proof Point to Reference (Message 2 only)
"Built a maintenance triage automation for a NYC property manager — tenant submits the request, n8n classifies urgency (emergency/routine/cosmetic), routes to the right vendor from a Google Sheet, sends the tenant a confirmation, and flags the owner only when a job is stalled. Manager went from 30 touchpoints a day to reviewing escalations only."

---

## Hook Signal Priority (use first available)
1. A LinkedIn post by the contact in the last 30 days (company milestone, team announcement, market take)
2. Unit count — more units = more intake volume = bigger pain
3. Software platform (AppFolio/Buildium/UnitConnect) — names the existing stack, signals you've worked with it
4. HPD violation history — operational signal (not a shaming opener; use to name the brand/ops gap)
5. Years in business — longevity = institutional knowledge trapped in people's heads

---

## n8n Language to Use (mandatory for PM niche)
Use at least one of these in M1 to signal technical credibility without pitching:
- "n8n intake workflow"
- "triage-to-dispatch automation"
- "classification layer before the vendor call"
- "automated routing to your vendor list"
- "n8n-based maintenance triage"
- "intake automation" (short form, acceptable)

DO NOT use: "AI solution," "workflow automation," "custom software," "built a system" — too generic.

---

## Subject Line Examples (by hook type)
- Unit count hook: "[X] units, one triage layer"
- Platform hook: "AppFolio intake without the manual step"
- Longevity hook: "[X] years of PM ops — still routing manually?"
- Violation hook: "HPD citations vs. your brand story"

---

## DM Template Structure

**Opener (1 sentence):** State a specific observable fact — unit count, platform, years in business, or structural reality. NOT a pain accusation.

**Curiosity bridge (1–2 sentences):** Drop a genuine question about how they handle a specific step in their intake flow. Reference n8n or automation language once. Peer-to-peer tone.

**Micro-CTA (1 sentence):** Open-ended observation question, not binary. "Is that kind of bottleneck something you're still dealing with?" NOT "Worth a call?"

---

## Example (60-unit AppFolio operator)

**Subject:** AppFolio intake — the step before the vendor call

**DM:**
Running 60 units on AppFolio means your maintenance intake volume is real — and the step between "tenant submits" and "vendor is dispatched" is usually still someone's job.

Been working with a similar NYC operator on an n8n triage layer that classifies urgency and routes to the right vendor automatically. Nothing changes about the workflow — just removes the manual touchpoint.

Is that kind of gap something your team is still navigating?

---

## What Makes a PM Hook Specific (not generic)
✅ "60 units on AppFolio — the intake step before vendor dispatch is usually still manual"
✅ "25 years managing properties in Brooklyn — that kind of institutional knowledge usually lives in one person's head"
✅ "Saw you're running both AppFolio and Buildium across portfolios — curious how intake gets routed between them"
❌ "Property managers spend too much time on maintenance" (generic pain)
❌ "I noticed you're a property manager" (segmentation, not personalization)
❌ "Your team must be overwhelmed with requests" (assumptive pain)
