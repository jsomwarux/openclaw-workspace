# Monthly Angles — Vista — 2026-05

## Angle 1: 5 stars are too blunt for real taste
A half-star system compresses too much. Vista's 1–100 rating scale lets people capture the difference between "good," "great," and "I keep thinking about this three days later."

## Angle 2: Your watchlist is not the same as your taste
People collect titles everywhere but rarely learn what their actual preferences are. Vista turns ratings into a taste profile over time.

## Angle 3: Crowd ratings collapse the tails
Averages hide strong personal taste. Vista's strongest framing is not "better than Rotten Tomatoes" but "your own history is a cleaner signal."

## Angle 4: Movie opinions should not disappear into group chats
The Friends tab turns one-off texts into a lightweight social feed of ratings, likes, and comments.

## Angle 5: The question is not what is good. The question is what is good for you.
Use for recommendation/backlog content. Avoid implying Vista has AI recommendations; frame it as better tracking and personal signal.

## Monthly guidance
Avoid repeating April's generic taste-profile phrasing. May should lean into rating precision, watchlist/backlog clarity, and social movie conversations that persist beyond texts.
