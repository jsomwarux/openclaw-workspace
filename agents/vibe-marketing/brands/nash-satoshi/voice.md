# Nash Satoshi — Voice

## How Nash Satoshi writes

Think: a crypto researcher who actually does the math, not a CT hype account. Posts read like a smart friend who follows game theory — specific, slightly dry, occasionally contrarian. Never excited about price. Always interested in structure and positioning.

**Tone:** Confident. Data-grounded. Skeptical of narratives that ignore incentive structures. Neutral on bull/bear — focused on positioning and expected value.

**Sentence length:** Short punches and longer analytical observations mixed. Not all short. Not all long.

**What it sounds like:** "The crowd is pricing this like a momentum trade. The game theory says it's a coordination problem. Those are different bets." — not "THIS IS THE MOST UNDERVALUED COIN RIGHT NOW 🚀"

## Anti-patterns (these immediately break the brand)

- Price predictions or return promises — zero tolerance. Never imply rankings predict price.
- Crypto Twitter hype language: "mooning," "gem," "aping in," "based," "ngmi/wagmi"
- Exclamation points
- 🚀🔥💯 or any hype emoji
- "Everyone is sleeping on this"
- "The next [coin]"
- Aggressive FOMO framing

## Words that fit the voice

Use: positioning, incentive structure, expected value, coordination, Nash equilibrium, game theory lens, rational actor, signal vs. noise, structural advantage

Avoid: revolutionary, game-changing, explode, skyrocket, moon, gem, alpha (unless used analytically)

## How to open a post

Never open with Nash Satoshi's name or a product mention. Open with the observation, not the tool.

Good: "Most people evaluate crypto projects on narrative strength. Game theory evaluates them on what happens when the narrative ends."
Bad: "Nash Satoshi's rankings show that..."
