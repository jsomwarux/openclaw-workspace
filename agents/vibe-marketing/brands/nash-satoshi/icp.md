# Nash Satoshi — ICP (Ideal Customer Profiles)

Three distinct audiences. Same product, different angle per ICP. When the ICP multiplier runs, it rewrites a winning script through each lens.

---

## ICP 1: The Analytical Holder

**Who:** 25–40, holds crypto seriously, reads research, doesn't trade on CT hype. More interested in thesis than momentum. Likely has a spreadsheet or framework they use to evaluate projects.

**Their problem:** Most crypto content is noise. Narrative-driven. They want a framework that cuts through the hype and evaluates structural positioning — not "will this moon?"

**What resonates:** Game theory as a framework. The idea that incentive structures predict behavior better than sentiment does. Intellectual respect — not excitement.

**Voice shift:** More analytical language. "Rational actors," "equilibrium," "coordination." Less hype, more framework.

**Hook angle:** "Most people evaluate crypto projects on narrative strength. Here's the question they're not asking."

---

## ICP 2: The Curious Skeptic

**Who:** 22–35, aware of crypto, not deep in it. Skeptical of hype but open to finding a signal that isn't momentum-based. Probably lost money on a narrative trade and is looking for something more grounded.

**Their problem:** Every crypto tool or influencer is just repackaged hype with a different frame. They want something that at least tries to be rigorous.

**What resonates:** The contrast with what already exists. "This isn't sentiment analysis" lands hard. Intellectual honesty — admitting what the model doesn't do (price prediction) actually builds trust.

**Voice shift:** More accessible language. Explain the game theory angle without assuming they know the term. Use analogy.

**Hook angle:** "Every crypto ranking I've seen is just repackaged hype. This one starts with a different question."

---

## ICP 3: The Builder / AI-Adjacent

**Who:** 28–45, works in tech or AI, finds the *methodology* interesting as much as the product. Interested in how a 4-LLM ensemble works, what the prompt architecture looks like, the operational side of building a ranking system.

**Their problem:** They want to see how AI is being applied to real, specific domains — not another chatbot. Nash Satoshi is interesting as a use case of AI applied to financial analysis.

**What resonates:** Behind-the-scenes methodology. The 4-LLM ensemble design. The choice of game theory as the framework. Build-in-public transparency.

**Voice shift:** More technical. Mention the ensemble. Explain why multiple models reduce single-model bias. Frame it as a system design decision.

**Hook angle:** "I built a 4-LLM ensemble to rank crypto projects by game theory positioning. Here's why single-model analysis breaks down."
