# Vista — Voice

## How Vista writes

Think: someone with actual opinions about movies — not a film snob, not a casual viewer. Posts read like the person in your group chat who texts "wait that movie was genuinely great, why does everyone hate it?" They track what they watch, notice patterns in their own taste, and find crowdsourced ratings kind of useless for personal recommendations.

**Tone:** Casual but specific. Personal taste as a legitimate signal. Mildly contrarian on crowd ratings — not dismissive, just skeptical of averages.

**Sentence length:** Short, punchy. This is a social app. Write like someone texting about a movie they just watched.

**What it sounds like:** "IMDb gives Annihilation a 6.8. My taste profile says I'd give it a 91. Those are different things." — not "Vista is a revolutionary movie tracking app that uses AI taste profiles to deliver personalized recommendations."

## Anti-patterns

- Product pitching in the opening line — never name Vista first
- Rotten Tomatoes/IMDb bashing — critique the *metric* not the platform
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," or any tech marketing language
- Trashing specific films or their creators
- Implying Vista has features it doesn't (e.g. don't promise AI recommendations if that's not live)
- Exclamation points
- 🎬🎥🍿 — feel free to use sparingly but don't lean on them

## Words that fit the voice

Use: taste, profile, pattern, tracked, ratings gap, personal signal, what the crowd misses

Avoid: algorithm, AI-powered, personalized recommendations (unless that's a confirmed feature), revolutionary, platform

## How to open a post

Never open with "Vista." Open with a movie observation, a rating gap, or a taste pattern the reader recognizes.

Good: "Rotten Tomatoes gave Hereditary 88%. My last 200 ratings tell me I'd give it a 94. Crowd averages miss the people with strong taste at the tails."
Bad: "Vista is a movie rating app that tracks your taste profile..."
