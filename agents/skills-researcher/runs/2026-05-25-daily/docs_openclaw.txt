<h2 id="what-is-openclaw%3F" tabindex="-1">What is OpenClaw?</h2>
<p>OpenClaw is a <strong>self-hosted gateway</strong> that connects your favorite chat apps and channel surfaces — built-in channels plus bundled or external channel plugins such as Discord, Google Chat, iMessage, Matrix, Microsoft Teams, Signal, Slack, Telegram, WhatsApp, Zalo, and more — to AI coding agents like Pi. You run a single Gateway process on your own machine (or a server), and it becomes the bridge between your messaging apps and an always-available AI assistant.</p>
<p><strong>Who is it for?</strong> Developers and power users who want a personal AI assistant they can message from anywhere — without giving up control of their data or relying on a hosted service.</p>
<p><strong>What makes it different?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Self-hosted</strong>: runs on your hardware, your rules</li>
<li><strong>Multi-channel</strong>: one Gateway serves built-in channels plus bundled or external channel plugins simultaneously</li>
<li><strong>Agent-native</strong>: built for coding agents with tool use, sessions, memory, and multi-agent routing</li>
<li><strong>Open source</strong>: MIT licensed, community-driven</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What do you need?</strong> Node 24 (recommended), or Node 22 LTS (<code>22.19+</code>) for compatibility, an API key from your chosen provider, and 5 minutes. For best quality and security, use the strongest latest-generation model available.</p>

<h2 id="configuration-(optional)" tabindex="-1">Configuration (optional)</h2>
<p>Config lives at <code>~/.openclaw/openclaw.json</code>.</p>
<ul>
<li>If you <strong>do nothing</strong>, OpenClaw uses the bundled Pi binary in RPC mode with per-sender sessions.</li>
<li>If you want to lock it down, start with <code>channels.whatsapp.allowFrom</code> and (for groups) mention rules.</li>
