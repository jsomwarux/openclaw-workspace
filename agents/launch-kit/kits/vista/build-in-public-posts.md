## Post 2: Why I Built This

**Platform: X**
**Format: 2-3 sentences. Honest frustration, no hype.**

---

Star ratings made every opinion feel the same. A film that rewired how I see the world sat at 5 stars next to a movie I'd half-forgotten by the next morning.

Vista uses 1-100. Now the difference shows.

---

**Alternate version:**

Letterboxd is great until you have 400 five-star films and can't figure out which ones you actually love most. Half-stars don't fix that.

So I built a 1-100 scale with a taste profile that ranks your films for you.

---

## Post 3: What Surprised Me

**Platform: X**
**Format: Specific, non-obvious insight. Not a feature announcement.**

---

The part I didn't expect: once users have a 1-100 scale, they start re-rating old films compulsively. Not just new ones.

Something about the precision makes people want to go back and actually decide. The star system lets you be vague. 1-100 forces a real opinion.

---

**Alternate version:**

Building the taste profile algorithm taught me something. Most people have seen the same 20 directors more than anyone else in their history, but they don't think of themselves as having a "top director."

Vista shows you that data. Watching people see their own patterns for the first time was the thing I didn't plan for.

---

## Usage Notes

- Post 1 goes out on launch day. One post only. Do not stack posts on the same day.
- Post 2 goes out 2-3 days after launch. Let Post 1 breathe.
- Post 3 goes out in Week 2, after the first real usage data comes in. Ideally grounded in something you actually observed from early users.
- None of these open with "Vista" or the product name. They open with the observation or the feeling. That's the rule.
- Do not add "Download Vista" at the end of Posts 1 and 2. The App Store link lives in the bio. Trust the reader to find it.
- Post 3 is the strongest for engagement because it's counterintuitive. Lead with it if you're pitching content to tech/dev Twitter circles.
- All three should be posted from @jts_14. Cross-post to LinkedIn as plain text (no hashtags on LinkedIn).
- No exclamation points. No em dashes. No "this is a game changer." Let the product speak.
- For LinkedIn: Add one sentence of professional context before each post. Example before Post 2: "Six months of nights and weekends. Here's what pushed me to build Vista in the first place."
