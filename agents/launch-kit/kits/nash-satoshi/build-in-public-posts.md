## LinkedIn version (JT's personal account — once per month)

**Angle:** Architecture Reveal format. Lead with the broken state, turning point, layer-by-layer build, paradox closer.

**Draft:**
Most crypto analysis is sentiment analysis with better branding.

I spent months thinking about what a structurally sound crypto asset actually looks like — not "will it go up" but "are the incentive structures coherent?"

Here's what I built:

Nash Satoshi runs every asset through a 4-LLM ensemble. Each model evaluates independently across game theory dimensions: defection-resistance, coordination stability, rational actor behavior, Nash equilibrium properties. Results aggregated, normalized, published as rankings.

The 4-model design wasn't aesthetic — single-model analysis produces overconfident outputs. When you run four models independently, disagreement between them becomes a signal. An asset three models rate highly and one flags as defection-prone is more interesting than one all four agree on.

What I've learned: correlation between market cap and game-theoretic score is weak enough to matter. High-attention assets often score poorly on coordination stability.

Live at nashsatoshi.com.
