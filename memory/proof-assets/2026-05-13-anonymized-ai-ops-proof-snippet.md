## Short LinkedIn proof angle
Most AI automation fails because it starts with the tool instead of the workflow.

For a NYC construction and real-estate operator, the useful system was not a chatbot.

It was:
- a live progress dashboard across 3 active job sites
- recurring StreetEasy research pushed into Google Sheets
- clear exception visibility instead of another inbox
- repeatable reporting the operator could trust

That is the real shape of AI implementation work: map the operating surface, find the stuck handoff, then automate the narrow part that removes drag.
