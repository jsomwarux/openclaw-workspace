# App Marketing OS — Content Feature Taxonomy

## Purpose
The measurement system should not only label winners and losers. It must extract *why* a post worked or failed, then feed those lessons back into future generation.

## How Future Generation Should Use This

For each product/platform:
1. Prefer hook techniques that produce repeated winners.
2. Prefer topics/content pillars with above-baseline performance.
3. Reuse the structure of winners, not their exact wording.
4. Retire formats/pillars that repeatedly underperform or violate platform norms.
5. If a winner relies on high specificity, future posts must include comparable specificity.
6. If a loser is vague/status-only, future posts must add proof, numbers, or tension.
7. Do not overfit one spike; require repeat signal or clear qualitative fit.

## Current Early Lessons

### Nash Satoshi
- Winning direction: model upgrades, updated rankings, specific token/ranking data, and methodology framed as a sharper analysis system.
- Weak direction: vague “watch this,” generic methodology fragments, and posts without concrete stakes.
- Generation implication: lead with specific model/ranking change, consensus rarity, or a concrete token/ranking insight. Avoid generic “game theory layer” explanations without proof.

### Vista
- Winning direction: relationship/social compatibility framing.
- Weak direction: generic rating-system complaints without enough emotional or social tension.
- Generation implication: movie taste as relationship/social signal is stronger than pure ratings precision. Keep precision, but wrap it in social identity/conflict.
