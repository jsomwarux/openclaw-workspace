## Page Copy

# How Nash Satoshi ranks crypto tokens

Nash Satoshi is a crypto research system that uses multiple AI models to analyze token positioning. It does not predict prices. It ranks tokens by looking at narrative strength, incentive design, coordination dynamics, liquidity context, and game-theory structure.

The goal is simple: make crypto research less dependent on hype, influencers, and one-model opinions.

## Why single-model crypto analysis is fragile

Single-model crypto analysis is easy to overtrust.

One model can inherit the framing of the prompt. One model can overweight recent hype. One model can miss incentive problems that another model catches.

Crypto is especially hard because token markets are reflexive. Narratives move prices, prices reinforce narratives, and incentives shape how holders, traders, builders, and communities behave.

That makes disagreement useful. If strong models disagree on a token, the disagreement itself is a research signal.

## The Nash Satoshi framework

Nash Satoshi uses a multi-model workflow to score tokens across several dimensions:

- narrative strength
- incentive design
- coordination dynamics
- liquidity and market structure context
- game-theory positioning
- durability of the thesis
- model consensus and disagreement

Each model analyzes the token independently. The system then compares where the models agree, where they disagree, and why.

A high ranking does not mean a token will go up. It means the token appears better positioned across the framework than lower-ranked alternatives.

## What “game-theory positioning” means

Game-theory positioning asks a different question than normal crypto analysis.

Instead of asking, “Is this token popular right now?” it asks:

- What are rational actors incentivized to do?
- Does the token create durable reasons to hold, use, build, or coordinate?
- Can the narrative survive once rewards, hype, or short-term momentum fade?
- Is the community coordinating around something structurally useful, or just reacting to price?

A token can have a strong product and weak incentives. It can have hype and weak coordination. It can have a compelling narrative that collapses when the game changes.

Nash Satoshi tries to surface those differences.

## What the rankings are for

The rankings are designed for research and comparison.

They help answer questions like:

- Which tokens have the strongest positioning right now?
- Where do multiple models agree?
- Where is model disagreement unusually high?
- Which narratives look strong but have incentive problems?
- Which tokens may be under-discussed relative to their structure?

The rankings are not financial advice, buy signals, sell signals, or price predictions.

## How to read a Nash Satoshi score

A Nash Satoshi score should be read as a positioning score, not a forecast.

A stronger score means the token performed better across the analysis framework. A weaker score means the models found more concerns, weaker positioning, or less durable structure.

The most useful part is often not the number itself. It is the reasoning behind the score:

- where the models agreed
- where they disagreed
- what risks appeared repeatedly
- which incentives looked durable
- which narratives looked fragile

## Why model consensus matters

Consensus matters because crypto analysis is noisy.

If one model likes a token, that may be interesting. If several models independently identify the same strengths and risks, the signal is stronger.

But consensus is not certainty. It is a way to reduce one-model blind spots and make the research process more structured.

## What Nash Satoshi is not

Nash Satoshi is not:

- price prediction
- financial advice
- a guarantee of returns
- a trading signal
- a replacement for independent research

It is a research layer for understanding crypto token positioning through a multi-model, game-theory lens.

## FAQ

### Does Nash Satoshi predict token prices?

No. Nash Satoshi does not predict prices. It ranks tokens by game-theory positioning, narrative strength, incentives, and model consensus.

### Is Nash Satoshi financial advice?

No. Nash Satoshi is a research tool. It should not be treated as financial advice or a buy/sell recommendation.

### Why use multiple AI models?

Multiple models reduce dependence on one model’s blind spots. Agreement between models can strengthen a signal, while disagreement can reveal risks or uncertainty.

### What makes a token rank highly?

A token ranks highly when it scores well across narrative, incentives, coordination, liquidity context, and game-theory positioning. Model consensus also matters.

### What should I do with model disagreement?

Model disagreement should be treated as a research prompt. It means the token may be ambiguous, polarizing, or dependent on assumptions that need closer inspection.
