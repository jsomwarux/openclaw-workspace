Title: In crypto ranking systems, would you weight trend movement or analysis recency more?

I am looking at a research-only crypto ranking snapshot where PUMPCADE is ranked #1 at 83.19 with a +26.44 trend move, and DOT is #2 at 83.13 with the freshest analysis timestamp in the top 10.

The gap is basically nothing.

That makes the methodology question more interesting than the rank.

If a ranking model is trying to stay useful in a noisy market, should it reward sharp trend movement more, or fresher analysis more?

My instinct is that neither should automatically win.

Trend movement can show where attention is shifting.
Freshness can matter when narratives change fast.
But either one can become noise if the system treats it like certainty.

The current top 10 also mixes prediction markets, AI, Solana gaming, stablecoin infrastructure, AI agents, RWAs, and decentralized compute, which matches the broader infrastructure narrative showing up in recent stablecoin/tokenization coverage.

I would not use this as a buy list. That is the wrong job for a leaderboard.

I would use it as a disagreement map:

- where is the model rewarding momentum?
- where is it rewarding freshness?
- where are categories converging even if the tokens look unrelated?

Curious how people here would design this. Should trend movement or analysis recency carry more weight in a crypto ranking model?
