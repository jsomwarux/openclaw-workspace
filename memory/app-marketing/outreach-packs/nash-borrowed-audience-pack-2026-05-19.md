## 6. Quality Check: Safety and Why These Are Not Spam

### Safety check

These drafts avoid:
- price targets,
- performance promises,
- buy/sell language,
- “undervalued gems” framing,
- “beat the market” framing,
- investment advice,
- implied partnerships,
- scraping or using another platform’s data without permission.

Every message frames Nash as a thesis/receipt artifact, not a trading signal.

### Why these are not spam

- The ask is narrow: critique or permission to send one useful asset.
- The asset is free and editorially useful.
- The drafts lead with the target’s audience needs, not “download my app.”
- The first batch is capped at 5 targets.
- Only 2 targets are allowed for soft feedback before public proof assets exist.
- Larger media targets are gated behind two public receipts and methodology proof.
- Each message has a target-specific reason to exist.

### Pre-send checklist

Before JT sends any message:
- [ ] Does the attached receipt stand alone without app context?
- [ ] Are there at least 5 projects/tokens on the card?
- [ ] Does every movement have a reason?
- [ ] Is model disagreement visible?
- [ ] Is there an invalidation condition or “what would change the thesis” note?
- [ ] Is the disclaimer visible?
- [ ] Does the message avoid price and performance promises?
- [ ] Does the target have the right gate status?
- [ ] Would JT be comfortable sending this with light edits?
