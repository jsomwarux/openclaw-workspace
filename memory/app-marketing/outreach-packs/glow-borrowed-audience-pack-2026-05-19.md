## 6. Quality check: claims safety and why these are not spam

### Claims safety

This pack stays inside Glow’s safe lane:
- Says “consumer product research,” not skin guidance.
- Uses “watch-items,” “fit flags,” “worth comparing,” and “price/value.”
- Avoids “safe,” “unsafe,” “toxic,” “clean,” “dirty,” “will clear,” “will treat,” “expert-approved,” and condition-safe language.
- No fake testimonials, no transformation-photo, no implied clinical endorsement.
- Credentialed/medical-adjacent targets are approached for format feedback only, not endorsement.
- SPF wording includes “follow label directions” and avoids efficacy claims beyond product category context.

### Why these are not spam

These messages are asset-led and target-specific:
- Each message references the target’s existing content format or audience behavior.
- The ask is low-friction: feedback on a card format, not a meeting or promotion.
- JT offers a useful artifact tied to products their audience already discusses.
- Reddit starts with mod permission, not a self-promo launch post.
- The first batch is capped at five targets.
- Follow-ups are limited and only add a better artifact.

### Final send/no-send rule

Send only if JT can attach or link a real artifact that would be useful even if the recipient never mentions Glow.

If the message depends on them caring about the app itself, do not send yet.
