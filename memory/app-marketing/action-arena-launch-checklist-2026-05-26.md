# Action Arena App Store Launch Marketing Checklist

Created: 2026-05-27
Task source: `Action Arena: prepare App Store launch marketing checklist`
Owner: Eve for planning/assets/tasks; JT for final App Store account actions, public posting, and any network outreach.

## Launch Posture

Action Arena should launch as a social sports prediction competition, not a gambling app.

Positioning:
- Action Arena is a fake-budget sports prediction league for friends.
- Players build weekly cards with straight picks, parlays, and teasers.
- The game rewards profit, discipline, and card construction.
- No real money is ever wagered.

Launch objective:
- Be App Store ready before approval.
- Capture beta/league interest before broad promotion.
- Use @dynastyjig as the trust-building sports-native channel.
- Avoid public claims that imply real-money betting, wagering advice, or gambling outcomes.

Do not do yet:
- Do not submit public directory listings until the App Store page or waitlist page exists.
- Do not run paid acquisition until store conversion assets are tested.
- Do not use generic TikTok/ReelFarm direct posting while account trust is unresolved.
- Do not promote Action Arena as a sportsbook, betting tips product, or gambling edge.

## Apple/App Store Guardrails

Current Apple-facing constraints to honor:
- Screenshots should show the app in use, not just title art or a splash screen.
- App previews should demonstrate real product behavior on-device.
- Metadata, screenshots, previews, and privacy information must accurately reflect the core experience.
- Product-page assets should be specific enough for review and not imply unsupported features.
- App Store badges, Apple product images, and App Store marketing assets must follow Apple identity guidelines.
- App Store Connect currently accepts one to ten screenshots per supported platform/localization.
- App previews are optional; if used, they should be 15 to 30 seconds, use accepted video formats, and be captured from real app use.

Source links:
- App Store Review Guidelines: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/
- App previews: https://developer.apple.com/support/app-previews/
- Upload screenshots/previews: https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/manage-app-information/upload-app-previews-and-screenshots/
- App Store marketing guidelines: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/marketing/guidelines/
- Product page guidance: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/product-page/

## App Store Submission Asset Checklist

Owner: Eve drafts; JT uploads/approves in App Store Connect.
Target timing: before final submission or immediately after final test build.

Required before upload:
- App name: `Action Arena`.
- Subtitle draft: `Fake-budget sports pick leagues`.
- Category recommendation: Games or Sports, depending on final App Store Connect fit.
- Description draft that states no real money is wagered in the first visible section.
- Keywords draft focused on fantasy football, sports picks, prediction league, fake-money betting game, parlay strategy, weekly competition.
- Privacy notes checked against real data collection.
- Review notes explaining the fake-budget mechanic, league flow, and no-real-money boundary.

Screenshot set:
Use five to seven screenshots for first submission unless the final build has enough distinct screens to justify more. Apple allows up to ten; do not fill all ten with repetitive screens.

1. League dashboard: show weekly standings, bankroll/profit, and matchup state.
2. Build your card: show straight picks, parlays, and teasers as game mechanics.
3. Fake budget: show the weekly `$100` fake budget and remaining allocation.
4. Matchup/leaderboard: show competition against friends.
5. Results/settlement: show profit/loss and weekly ranking.
6. Chat/social proof if built: show league trash-talk or shared pick card without real-money claims.

Screenshot copy rules:
- Use product screens underneath every marketing headline.
- Keep copy short and benefit-led.
- Avoid "win money," "beat the book," "guaranteed," "locks," "sharp picks," or "profit from betting."
- Use "fake budget," "prediction league," "card construction," "friends," "weekly competition," and "no real money."

Suggested screenshot headlines:
- `Build the sharpest weekly card`
- `Compete with a fake $100 budget`
- `Straight picks, parlays, and teasers`
- `Beat your friends on profit`
- `Track every weekly result`
- `Sports strategy. No real money.`

App preview video:
- Optional for first submission, but valuable after the product is stable.
- 15 to 30 seconds.
- Use only real product capture or a faithful App Store-safe recording; no placeholder UI.
- Show: create/join league, build card, allocate fake budget, view matchup, see result.
- No hype-only montage. The video should demonstrate actual app usage.

## Prelaunch Positioning Checklist

Owner: Eve drafts; JT approves.
Target timing: before App Store approval.

One-sentence positioning:
`Action Arena turns sports picks into a weekly league with friends: build a card, manage a fake budget, and compete on profit without wagering real money.`

Short App Store opening:
`Action Arena is a social sports prediction game where friends compete with a weekly fake budget. Build cards with straight picks, parlays, and teasers, then see who manages risk and profit best. No real money is ever wagered.`

Audience:
- Fantasy football players who already talk weekly matchups and picks.
- Sports fans who like parlays but want a no-money strategy game.
- Group chats that want a weekly competition layer.
- @dynastyjig followers who respond to betting-card discipline and fantasy strategy content.

Promise:
- Compete on strategy, not bankroll size.
- Turn weekly sports arguments into a league.
- Practice card construction without financial risk.

Proof needed before stronger launch claims:
- Real app screenshots.
- One clean beta league flow.
- At least one completed sample week or simulated week.
- App Store approval status.
- Any early TestFlight/beta interest signal.

Banned patterns:

- "Locks," guaranteed wins, or betting edge claims.
- Product-first spam in sports communities.
- Trend-locked slideshow hooks that require original TikTok audio/context.
- Viewer-interactive hooks that promise comments/DMs before the app is visible.

## Launch-Day Checklist

Do not launch publicly until:
- App Store link or waitlist link exists.
- First five screenshots are final or good enough.
- Fake-money/no-real-money language is visible in App Store description and launch copy.
- First-week metrics sheet has fields ready.
- JT has a short support list of 20 people who can download, join, or share.
- At least one beta league path is clear.

Launch-day sequence:
1. App Store live check.
2. Update waitlist/page CTA to App Store link if live.
3. Post @dynastyjig launch thread or single strong launch post.
4. Post one JT personal account builder note only if useful.
5. Send private support asks to known sports/fantasy friends. JT sends, not Eve.
6. Log App Store product page views/downloads if available.
7. Log @dynastyjig post metrics at 24h, 72h, and 7d.

Everyone gets a fake weekly budget.
Build a card with straight picks, parlays, and teasers.
Compete on profit against your friends.

No real money. Just strategy, accountability, and bragging rights.`

## First-Week Metrics

Owner: Eve tracks from JT-provided/app-source metrics; JT supplies App Store Connect numbers if needed.

Required fields:
- App Store product page views.
- App Store downloads.
- App Store conversion rate if available.
- App Store ratings/reviews.
- Waitlist signups or beta league requests.
- Number of created leagues.
- Number of joined users.
- Number of completed cards.
- @dynastyjig impressions, likes, replies, saves, profile clicks.
- TikTok/ReelFarm 24h/72h/7d metrics if any posts resume.

Decision rules:
- If downloads happen but league creation is weak, improve onboarding and first-league CTA.
- If product page views are low, improve distribution and support asks.
- If product page views are high but downloads are low, revise screenshots/subtitle.
- If beta interest is mostly from group-chat/fantasy people, prioritize private leagues over broad public promo.
- If real-money interpretation appears in replies, tighten copy immediately.
