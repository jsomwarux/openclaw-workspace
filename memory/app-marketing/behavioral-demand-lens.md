# App Marketing OS — Behavioral Demand Lens

Created: 2026-05-10

## Purpose
Improve passive-income/app idea selection and marketing by identifying the human motive that makes someone care, click, save, share, buy, or return.

This is not dark-pattern marketing. It is a product-demand filter: useful ideas still fail when no one feels urgency.

## Core Question
What human urge makes someone care about this before they need it?

## Demand Motives
Use these as ethical lenses:
- **Taste identity:** “This reflects my standards.”
- **Status:** “This makes me look sharper / earlier / more discerning.”
- **Fear of waste:** “I do not want to waste money, time, attention, or effort.”
- **Control/certainty:** “I want to feel less confused.”
- **Embarrassment avoidance:** “I do not want to make a dumb public/private choice.”
- **Curiosity/comparison:** “I want to know which side wins.”
- **Self-improvement:** “This helps me become the version of myself I want.”
- **Belonging:** “People like me care about this.”
- **Convenience/time recovery:** “This removes a recurring hassle.”
- **Buyer protection/skepticism:** “This helps me avoid hype or bad incentives.”

## Product Examples
### Vista
Weak: “Rate movies from 1–100.”
Stronger: “Your favorite movie should not get the same 4 stars as your comfort rewatch.”
Psychology: taste identity, precision, disagreement, comparison.

### Glow Index
Weak: “AI skincare rankings.”
Stronger: “Most skincare rankings reward hype. This one punishes weak evidence.”
Psychology: buyer protection, skepticism, control, fear of waste.

### Nash Satoshi
Weak: “Crypto rankings from four models.”
Stronger: “If four models disagree, that is the signal.”
Psychology: uncertainty reduction, contrarian confidence, pattern recognition.
