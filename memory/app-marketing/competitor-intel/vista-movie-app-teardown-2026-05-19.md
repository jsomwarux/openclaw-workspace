# Vista Movie App Competitor Teardown
Date: 2026-05-19
App: Vista
Scope: Letterboxd, Serializd, TV Time, Likewise, Sequel, JustWatch, IMDb, movie recommendation communities

## Executive Read
The movie/TV tracking category is crowded, but the incumbents mostly cluster around four jobs:
1. Log what you watched.
2. Find where to watch something.
3. Discover recommendations.
4. Follow friends/reviews/lists.

Vista’s wedge should be narrower: **precision ratings become taste identity, then taste identity becomes comparison.** Do not fight Letterboxd on social network depth. Use Vista’s 1–100 ratings to create artifacts that Letterboxd-style users already know how to share: taste cards, top-four cards, compatibility cards, and recap/list formats.

## Competitors Inspected

### Letterboxd
Sources:
- https://letterboxd.com/
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/letterboxd/id1054271011

Positioning:
- “Social film discovery.”
- Track watched films, save watchlist, tell friends what’s good.
- Social network for film lovers.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Friend/follower feed.
- Reviews, likes, comments.
- Lists and public profiles.
- Five-star ratings.
- Stats and profile identity for Pro/Patron.
- Cultural behavior around four favorites/top-four identity.

Evidence:
- App Store: 88K ratings, 4.8, Editors’ Choice.
- Homepage shows massive public activity, including billions of films watched.
- App Store copy highlights sharing lists/reviews and commenting on friends’ updates.

Gap for Vista:
- Letterboxd owns broad social film logging. Vista should not claim to replace it.
- Letterboxd’s rating input is five-star with halves. Vista can own finer-grained taste expression and make precision visible.

### Serializd
Sources:
- https://www.serializd.com/
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/serializd/id1581244120

Positioning:
- “For fans of TV... all of it.”
- Track, rate, review TV shows.
- Community/discussion around episodes and shows.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Friends/following.
- Reviews after episodes.
- Member-generated lists.
- Recommendations from members.

Evidence:
- App Store: 1K ratings, 4.5.
- Review language shows users invite others and want better social feeds, private/public lists, pinned lists, direct friend recommendations, and easier access to friends’ profiles.

Gap for Vista:
- Social feed demand is real, but hard to build from zero.
- Vista should start with share artifacts and invite loops rather than trying to build an empty feed.

### TV Time
Sources:
- https://www.tvtime.com/
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/tv-time-track-shows-movies/id431065232
- https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.tozelabs.tvshowtime&hl=en_US

Positioning:
- Track shows and movies.
- Organize what you watch.
- Reactions/trending behavior.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Large user-base claim in App Store search result: 25M+ people.
- Show reactions, tracking, community discussion.
- Trending shows by reactions.

Evidence:
- TV Time site surfaces trending shows with most reactions in last 3 days.
- Search result snippets show users value tracking and talking to others about shows.

Gap for Vista:
- TV Time’s value is utility + reactions. Vista can use rating precision to create higher-signal identity artifacts, not compete on show tracking breadth.

### Likewise / Pix
Sources:
- https://likewise.com/

Positioning:
- Entertainment recommendations across TV, movies, books, podcasts.
- Newsletter-led recommendation engine.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Large newsletter surfaces under Pix Media.
- TV & Movie Pix claims 1.5M subscribers.
- Recommendation lists and recurring editorial issues.

Evidence:
- Likewise/Pix page shows newsletter archive, “TV & Movie Pix,” and subscriber claim.

Gap for Vista:
- Likewise owns editorial “what to watch” distribution. Vista should pitch taste-card/list assets to roundup/newsletter writers rather than trying to become another generic recommendation app.

### Sequel
Sources:
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/sequel-media-tracker/id1630746993

Positioning:
- Media wishlist/tracker across TV, movies, books, games.
- Beautiful personal organization.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Press quotes from Snazzy Labs, MacStories, Christopher Lawley.
- iCloud/Trakt sync.
- Widgets, reminders, Magic Lookup/AI.

Evidence:
- App Store: 1.1K ratings, 4.8.
- Review language praises simple organization and replacing scattered tools.

Gap for Vista:
- Sequel is a polished personal tracker, not a social movie-taste identity layer. Vista should avoid multi-media scope and stay emotionally sharper.

### JustWatch
Sources:
- https://www.justwatch.com/us
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/justwatch-movies-tv-shows/id979227482

Positioning:
- Streaming guide: what to watch and where to watch it.

Visible acquisition loops:
- SEO landing pages for streaming availability.
- Provider pages and filters.
- Watchlist notifications when titles arrive on services.
- Press/social proof.

Evidence:
- App Store: 28K ratings, 4.7.
- App Store description claims 4,500+ providers globally and 90,000+ movies/TV shows.
- Press quotes from Gizmodo, BGR, NYT, Product Hunt, TechCrunch.

Gap for Vista:
- JustWatch is utility/search-intent. Vista should integrate “where to watch” only if easy; it is not the acquisition wedge.

### IMDb
Sources:
- https://apps.apple.com/us/app/imdb-movies-tv-shows/id342792525

Positioning:
- Tickets, trailers, ratings, entertainment guide.
- Personalized recommendations and watchlists.

Visible acquisition loops:
- Massive brand authority.
- Search and database utility.
- Watchlist, alerts, ratings.

Evidence:
- App Store: 546K ratings, 4.8.
- Review language requests better plot/character/actor search and subgenre filters.

Gap for Vista:
- IMDb owns broad factual lookup. Vista should not compete on database breadth. Vista can convert “what my ratings mean” into a personal/friend artifact.

## Acquisition Channels Visible
1. **Social graph:** Letterboxd, Serializd, TV Time rely on friend/follow behavior.
2. **Public identity artifacts:** profiles, lists, stats, reviews, top-four/favorites.
3. **Utility SEO:** JustWatch owns where-to-watch search; IMDb owns title/person lookup.
4. **Press/review credibility:** Sequel and JustWatch use press quotes.
5. **Newsletter/editorial distribution:** Likewise/Pix has TV/movie newsletters.
6. **Community prompts:** Reddit users ask for recommendations based on top four, similar taste, stats, and profile visualizations.

## User Complaints and Exact Language Themes
Use as directional themes, not as universal claims.

- Serializd users want stronger friend activity: chronological updates like “Friend started watching,” “Friend rated,” “Friend wrote a review.” Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/serializd/id1581244120
- Serializd users ask for private/public lists, pinned lists, better log/review history, privacy options, and less crashing/slow loading. Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/serializd/id1581244120
- IMDb users want better search by plot, character, actor descriptions, subgenres, year, and findability. Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/imdb-movies-tv-shows/id342792525
- Reddit users ask for film taste compatibility tests, similar-taste users, recommendations based on top four, and profile statistics/visualizations. Sources: https://www.reddit.com/r/Letterboxd/comments/lp06yi/what_would_you_like_to_see_in_a_film_taste/ and https://www.reddit.com/r/Letterboxd/comments/u9qghv/is_there_a_way_to_find_people_with_similar_tastes/

## Borrowed Audience Opportunities
Best fits:
- FilmTok creators who already ask people to share top four/favorites.
- Reddit communities where users request recommendations from favorites.
- Indie app reviewers who like polished niche apps.
- Film newsletters/roundups that need interactive prompts.
- Student/college film blogs where “your movies say who you are” framing fits.

Pitch asset, not app:
- “I made a 1–100 movie taste card for people who think five stars are too blunt.”
- “Can I make a version for your audience’s top-four challenge?”
- “Would a compatibility prompt work for your movie-night community?”

## Gaps JT Can Exploit
1. **Precision as identity:** 1–100 ratings make the card visibly different from star-based apps.
2. **Compatibility over social feed:** Better for an early app because it creates invite pressure without needing a huge network.
3. **Top-four remix:** Existing behavior already exists; Vista adds numeric precision.
4. **Stats without Pro complexity:** A lightweight taste card gives users the emotional value of stats without heavy dashboarding.
5. **Borrowed community prompts:** Many communities already ask “what should I watch based on X?” Vista can supply a structured asset.

## What Not To Do
- Do not call Vista a Letterboxd killer.
- Do not build a full social feed before there is enough network density.
- Do not lead with generic recommendations.
- Do not copy Letterboxd wording or UI metaphors.
- Do not fabricate user counts, compatibility stories, or testimonials.

## Recommendation
Build the **Movie Taste Card** first, but design it as the foundation for compatibility. The killer loop is not “share my profile.” It is: **rate movies → get taste card → compare with a friend → friend installs to generate their side.**
