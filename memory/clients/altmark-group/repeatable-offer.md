# Altmark Group — Repeatable Offer + Skill-Building Plan

Created: 2026-05-02

## Core Offer Pattern
Altmark is the template for a repeatable AI operations implementation offer for NYC family offices, real estate operators, property managers, and finance-heavy SMBs.

Positioning: self-hosted, audit-trailed automation for back-office workflows where the company already has messy but valuable operational data across spreadsheets, AppFolio, QuickBooks Desktop/Enterprise, inboxes, banking reports, and tenant/property systems.

## What JT Is Building For Altmark
- Foundation infrastructure: local/self-hosted automation server, secure internal access, AppFolio + QuickBooks + email/SMS integration paths, logging, rollback, and access controls.
- Insurance expiration tracking: daily spreadsheet scan, configurable expiration reminders, overdue flags, and daily summary.
- Loan management: document intake/extraction, lender/date/rate/payment/deadline tracking, reminders, and reporting.
- WhatsApp AI bot: conversational access to tenant/property/financial info with permission boundaries and audit logs.
- Flagstar to QuickBooks automation: statement/report ingestion, transaction classification, review queue, and QuickBooks update flow.
- Cash management + overdraft prevention: daily bank/cash checks, threshold alerts, timing risk flags, and recommended transfers requiring human approval.
- Rent delinquency outreach: AppFolio report ingestion, delinquency detection, personalized email/SMS outreach, cooldowns, dry run, and sent-summary reporting.

## Pricing Anchor From Proposal
- Foundation: $4,000
- Use cases: $2,250–$9,000 each
- Total proposed buildout: $34,750
- Foundation + first use case live within ~2 weeks after infrastructure session
- Full six-use-case buildout: ~8–10 weeks

## Best-Fit Prospect Profile
Prioritize prospects with:
1. Real estate, family office, property management, private lending, or asset-heavy operations.
2. Back-office workflows dependent on AppFolio, QuickBooks Desktop/Enterprise, spreadsheets, inbox reports, banking portals, or SMS/email follow-up.
3. A CFO/controller/operator feeling manual reporting, compliance, collections, renewal, or cash-timing pain.
4. Enough workflow volume that saving 5–15 hours/week or reducing missed deadlines is obviously valuable.
5. Low appetite for SaaS replacement; stronger fit when they need automation around existing systems.

## Prospecting Angles
- “You probably don’t need new software. You need the systems you already use to talk to each other.”
- “I build internal AI operations workflows for real estate/family-office teams: insurance expirations, delinquency outreach, QuickBooks/bank reconciliation, cash-risk alerts.”
- “Self-hosted, audit-trailed, no autonomous financial actions — the workflow flags and prepares, your team approves.”

## Skill Sharpening Priorities
1. n8n/self-hosted automation reliability: deployment, credentials, audit logs, error handling, dry-run modes.
2. QuickBooks Desktop/Enterprise integration patterns and safe review queues.
3. AppFolio data extraction/report ingestion patterns.
4. Secure internal architecture for nontechnical SMB offices: local server, remote access, least privilege, logs.
5. Workflow UX: operator approval screens, exception handling, summaries, and rollback.
6. Case-study packaging: before/after workflow map, time saved, risks removed, and repeatable offer page.

## Proactive Operating Rule
Whenever Altmark progress changes, convert the learning into three assets:
1. Client delivery note: what was built, blocker, fix, and reusable pattern.
2. Sales asset: one proof point or prospecting angle.
3. Skill gap: one concrete build/practice task that improves JT’s ability to sell and deliver the next similar client.
