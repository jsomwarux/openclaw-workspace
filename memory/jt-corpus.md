### 12. $85K Real Estate Automation Proof

- Platform: LinkedIn
- Format tag: proof post
- Date: original date unknown; supplied by JT on 2026-06-11
- Source: JT-supplied LinkedIn proof post

The CFO of a NYC real estate firm I work with just posted that the AI automation I built for his team is saving them $85K a year.

Here's what the workflow actually does:

Their portfolio has hundreds of commercial tenants, each contractually required to carry insurance and provide a Certificate of Insurance proving it. Tracking who's compliant, who's expired, and who needs follow-up was a full-time job spread across multiple people.

I built an automated system that runs on a dedicated machine in their office, connected to their existing tools. Every morning at 7am, it scans the entire portfolio, identifies who needs outreach, sends personalized emails on behalf of the correct ownership entity, and escalates through a multi-stage follow-up sequence. If a tenant doesn't respond, the system keeps following up weekly until they do.

Upper management gets a daily summary of who's compliant, who's lapsing, who's been contacted, and what needs a human.

The results after 3 weeks live:

Portfolio insurance compliance went from 11% to 78%. The team went from manually chasing tenants every day to only getting involved on real exceptions.

A few things that made this work:

-No SaaS subscription or vendor lock-in. The client owns the infrastructure and every workflow. If we part ways, everything keeps running.
-It connects to their existing systems. No migration, no new platforms to learn.
-Human-in-the-loop on anything sensitive. The system flags and recommends. The team decides.

This is one use case at one firm. I'm building five more automations for the same client, and expanding to other family offices in their network.

As my client put it: "In 5 years, the firms that are still doing this with humans won't exist. And for the ones already automating: this is the worst the technology will ever be."

If you manage a real estate portfolio and your team is still manually tracking insurance, lease renewals, rent collections, or vendor compliance, let's talk.
