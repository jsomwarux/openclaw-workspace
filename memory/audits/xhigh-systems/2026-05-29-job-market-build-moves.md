# 2026-05-29 Job Market Build/Consulting Moves Audit

## Read
The current job-market signal is not "more applications." It is that non-coding AI enablement roles are converging around operating systems: intake/front door, connectors, MCP-style tool access, eval harnesses, data lineage, governance, Champions/adoption mechanics, rollback standards, and ROI reporting.

Today's H.I.G. Capital Director, AI Enablement posting is the cleanest example. It scores 21/25 and is live, but the gaps are real: private-equity workflow depth, production platform ownership, and small technical-team leadership. Treat it as dual-track only, not a simple apply-now task.

## Optimal Moves
1. **Package proof before chasing roles.** The strongest near-term asset is still Altmark acceptance/proof. It proves governed operational AI with real dollars saved. Do not let speculative job-market demos outrank the rent delinquency gate.
2. **Turn H.I.G. into an executive-facing proof artifact.** Build the DealDesk AI Control Plane plan first, then decide whether to build. The artifact should show workflow mapping, connector/data-lineage assumptions, eval/rollback gates, adoption dashboard, and ROI hypotheses.
3. **Use the consulting bridge as a review-only packet.** H.I.G. gets an ACE Readiness Sprint note, not cold outreach from Eve. The packet should avoid PE expertise claims and lead with AI enablement operations.
4. **Consolidate duplicate build ideas.** AdoptAI, ROIFlow, AgentOps, ChampionOps, EnablementOps, and DealDesk are variants of the same market demand. The board should prioritize one combined "AI Enablement OS" proof lane instead of creating a new build for every role title.
5. **Create future job-scan tasks only when they name the move.** A good job-derived task must specify one of: apply package, consulting bridge packet, build/demo artifact, skill/agent to create, or market-intel note. Generic "review role" cards are insufficient.

## Current Board Actions
- Sharpened `Dual-track H.I.G. Capital — apply + consulting bridge` into a decision task with two outputs: application go/no-go and ACE Readiness Sprint packet.
- Sharpened `Build idea: DealDesk AI Control Plane — front-office AI platform ops` into a spec-first proof task tied directly to H.I.G. and enterprise AI enablement roles.
- Left older TelcoAgent, Ramp, Agentforce Contact Center, and WEF cards below the current proof/revenue layer. They are not wrong; they are not today's highest-leverage move.

## Operating Rule
For every future role scored 18+/25, extract:
- hiring pain
- route
- proof gap
- best build/demo/skill/consulting move
- whether the move is new or already covered by an existing artifact
- one concrete Mission Control task only if it beats or strengthens the current proof layer
