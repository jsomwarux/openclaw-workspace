## Wednesday - AI Ops Teardown
**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Pillar:** Wholesale Distribution Operations
**Source:** memory/content/bank/2026-06-01/ai-ops-teardown-canals-wholesale-order-intake.md; Unite.AI 2026-05-29 Canals $35M funding signal
**From seed:** false

Canals just raised $35M around a problem every wholesale distributor recognizes: messy orders.

One buyer emails a PDF. Another sends a spreadsheet. A rep forwards a voice note. A handwritten line item shows up with the wrong unit count.

The ERP still needs a clean order, price check, inventory check, exception owner, and confirmation back to the customer.

The workflow I would build starts with a shared order-intake desk.

It reads emails, PDFs, spreadsheets, and notes. It extracts customer, SKU, quantity, unit, requested date, and attachments. Then it checks catalog data, customer-specific pricing, inventory, and margin rules.

When the order is clean, it drafts the ERP-ready entry.

When something is off, it routes the exception to the right owner with the source attached.

Customers keep sending orders the way they already do. The distributor gets a cleaner intake layer before bad data hits the ERP.

## Sunday - Behind the Build
**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Pillar:** Property Management Operations
**Source:** memory/content/weekly-intel-brief.md, property management AI front desk/routing trend
**From seed:** false

Property management AI gets real at the intake desk.

A tenant email, a missed-call transcript, a vendor note, a lease date, and an owner rule should not become five separate follow-up systems.

The first useful workflow reads the request, checks the record, names the owner, and separates routine follow-up from anything that needs manager approval.

That is where the AI implementation actually starts:

- what came in
- what property it belongs to
- what detail is missing
- who owns the next step
- what should never leave without human approval

The buyer does not need a louder inbox.

They need a cleaner handoff before the wrong person replies, the wrong vendor gets routed, or the same issue gets followed up three times.

The implementation work is mapping the intake, defining the stop rules, connecting the records, and keeping humans in the decisions that can cost money.

**Checklist:** buyer-recognizable property management workflow, current weekly signal, concrete intake scene, no proof-hygiene/process-meta angle, no em dashes, no hashtags.
