## Wednesday Post
Every NYC property manager I talk to has the same problem.

They find out about delays after the fact. A job runs over. Nobody tells the client until the next morning. By then, the client's already noticed. Three calls, two voicemails, one frustrated client.

So I built around what already happens. When a job wraps, the foreman sends one WhatsApp message. Doesn't change their routine. Doesn't need an app or training. The system reads it, classifies the status, and sends the client a same-day update automatically.

The PM stopped chasing updates. The client stopped waiting.

That's it. That's the whole build.

The best part: field crew changes nothing about how they work. The business just starts running smoother underneath them.

The real unlock with automation isn't replacing your people. It's removing the administrative weight that keeps them from doing their actual job.
