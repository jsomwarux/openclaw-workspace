## Strategic Theme This Week

The strongest buyer angle is controlled execution before automation.

For JT's content, the market is asking for the operating record around AI work:

- source record
- allowed action
- approval owner
- held exception
- tool-call log
- rollback path
- measurable outcome

The Altmark rent-delinquency gate is still the cleanest concrete example because it forces the right question: before any tenant-sensitive follow-up, can the workflow prove which export started the run, which rows stopped, who reviewed them, and where the final decision landed?

## Competitive Developments

Real estate AI vendors are packaging orchestration, recommendations, and workflow automation with governance/audit language. The practical response is not to out-demo them. It is to sell the narrow first-workflow activation layer that an SMB can actually adopt: one workflow, one source export, one reviewer, one exception rule set, one logged outcome.

Agent-governance vendors and open-source tools are making MCP/tool access inspectable. JT's angle should turn that from hidden infrastructure into the proof artifact a buyer can understand: "Here is what the agent could access, what it requested, what was allowed or denied, who approved it, and where the evidence lives."

## Recommended Content Direction

Use the phrase family "operating record," "control record," "review-only run," and "tool-call ledger." Avoid generic "AI transformation" and "AI collections."
