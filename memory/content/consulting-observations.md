[2026-03-17] construction — Two consecutive commercial HVAC firms (Premier HVAC, Henick-Lane) both surfaced dispatcher disorganization through employee reviews on Indeed/Yelp — not job postings, not owner statements, but worker-generated signal. "Very disorganized and inexperienced dispatcher making everyone's life harder" and "dispatcher left." For construction/HVAC T2 prospecting, Indeed employee reviews are a more reliable pain signal than job postings. (Source: Premier HVAC Services, Henick-Lane Inc)

[2026-03-18] property management — PM firms running dual platforms (AppFolio for tenants + Buildium for owners simultaneously) have a more specific automation pitch than single-platform shops: the gap isn't missing software, it's the manual handoff between two live systems. Job-to-be-done is explicit and bounded, not an open-ended process question.

[2026-03-21] PROPERTY MANAGEMENT — PM firms that actively market their tech stack (AppFolio, Buildium, UnitConnect) as client value-adds are signaling the exact same gap: the tool is live, the automation layer isn't. Three separate operators tonight (ProRealty running two platforms simultaneously, FCM on UnitConnect at 60+ properties, Atlas NYC on Buildium with 44 open violations) all publicly market their systems while running manual coordination in the middle.

[2026-03-22] PROPERTY MANAGEMENT — Firms that explicitly market their team's operational efficiency as a competitive differentiator (testimonials praising 'streamlined to-do lists,' or branded identity built around systematic management) are the easiest AI pitch: they've already internalized the value of automation. The gap isn't belief in the concept — it's that they hit scale (1,000 units, dedicated coordinator hire) and the manual bridge still exists between what their PM software captures and what vendors actually do. MyPropertyMan's own testimonial language ('They streamline to do lists like no one') shows they're selling on a value they haven't fully delivered yet.

[2026-03-23] WHOLESALE DISTRIBUTION — 110-year-old wholesale electrical distributor (G&G Electric, Manhattan) with 36 employees, no online ordering portal, and a contractor customer base that still calls in to check availability. At this size and age, the catalog knowledge is entirely human-held. The pitch isn't "go digital" — it's "your catalog already exists, your customers just can't reach it without calling." The same pattern appears in HVAC parts (H.C. Oswald), electrical (G&G), and plumbing supply (Webster, Jasco) — established distributors whose value is inventory depth, not price, but who lose orders to whoever answers the phone fastest. (Source: G&G Electric Supply Co., Inc. — 2026-03-23 research)

[2026-03-24] FOLLOW-UP TIMING — The first 72 hours after a LinkedIn connection request sends are the highest-reply window. Eight prospects (construction, PM, wholesale, insurance) all sent M1 on 2026-03-23. M2 window is 2026-03-26 to 2026-03-27. At 8 concurrent prospects all in the same M2 window, the volume risk is sending too many connection notes in a single day — LinkedIn flags unusual connection-request bursts. JT should stagger these across 2 days (4 on Wed, 4 on Thu) rather than sending all 8 in one session. This is a consistent pattern: batched M1 sends create batched M2 windows that need to be paced.

[2026-03-27] CONSTRUCTION/HVAC — Commercial HVAC operations with multi-crew dispatch at 30-80 employee scale (AFGO Mechanical, Henick-Lane, Premier HVAC) consistently show the same pattern: two Service Managers or dispatch coordinators are added as headcount to solve a coordination problem that scales by crew count. The admin-hire-as-bottleneck-fix appears in job postings, website org charts, and employee reviews across all three. The real tell: firms hiring "administrative support for the sales team" or listing 2+ service managers by name are solving coordination with people, not systems. That's the AI pitch entry point. (Source: AFGO Mechanical Services research 2026-03-25)

[2026-03-28] CONSTRUCTION/TRADES — Dual-trade contractors (plumbing + HVAC, or HVAC + fire safety) have a more complex dispatch problem than single-trade shops: every job potentially involves two separate trade crews, two parts catalogs, and two scheduling queues. Maxwell Plumb ($16.4M, 30 emp, Bayside Queens) runs commercial plumbing + HVAC + fire safety 24/7 across all 5 boroughs with a live-operator dispatch model. The pitch to a dual-trade shop is fundamentally different from single-trade HVAC — the automation layer has to separate emergency from scheduled AND route by trade type simultaneously. This is a more specific pain than "dispatch is slow" and maps directly to the construction-job-tracker template's priority + trade routing logic. (Source: Maxwell Plumb Mechanical Corp research 2026-03-28)

[2026-03-29] PROPERTY MANAGEMENT — Tech-forward PM firms that explicitly market "real-time vendor coordination" and "seamless coordination between residents, staff, and vendors" (HPM's exact Yelp language) are the easiest AI pitch: they've already articulated the value, the gap is between the aspiration and what the portal actually does. HPM markets a mobile app + online portal + real-time project tracking, but the vendor routing and status update layer after intake is still manual. The brand promise creates the opening — the pitch doesn't have to sell the concept, only the delivery mechanism. This pattern holds across three PM firms now: HPM, MyPropertyMan ("streamline to-do lists"), Atlas NYC ("proactive management"). (Source: HPM Harlem Property Management research 2026-03-29)

[2026-03-30] CONSTRUCTION — Multi-sector GCs (hospitals + schools + tenant improvements + facility maintenance + emergency restoration) have a fundamentally different tracking problem than single-sector contractors: each client type brings its own compliance cadence, reporting format, and inspection schedule. G-Net Construction runs 7 service lines simultaneously with 65 years of embedded manual process. The architect/principal (Vincent Nativo, RA/AIA/LEED-AP) personally manages permit review — which means the coordination overhead from mixed project types lands directly on the most credentialed person in the firm. Automating job classification by type and compliance cadence is the pitch: not "your tracking is slow" but "your tracking treats hospital jobs and school jobs the same." (Source: G-Net Construction Corp research 2026-03-30)
