[2026-04-03] INSURANCE / AGENTFORCE — After 3+ weeks of insurance T3 Salesforce verification passes, a clear pattern holds: NYC-area independent brokerages under 50 employees almost universally run commodity web stacks (WordPress, GoDaddy DNS, PHP, MailChimp). Salesforce adoption in this segment correlates with two specific triggers: (1) PE acquisition or network affiliation (Patriot, Alera, Gallagher) = centralized CRM rollout, or (2) rapid growth that outpaces spreadsheet coordination + a named Salesforce admin appears on LinkedIn. Confirmed tonight: Glenridge Brokerage (Queens) — no Salesforce; Insurance Agency Group of NY (Manhattan) — ZoomInfo stack: Bullhorn/DealerOn/GoDaddy, no Salesforce; RMS Insurance Brokerage (Garden City) — AdRoll/RSS/jQuery only. Actionable rule: don't queue insurance T3 prospects for Salesforce verification unless they have a PE/network affiliation signal or a named CRM admin on LinkedIn first. Cold-verification of random independents consistently returns HOLD. (Source: pipeline verification runs 2026-03-24 through 2026-04-03)

[2026-04-02] OUTREACH SEQUENCING — At the M2-to-M4 stage, the pipeline splits into two distinct failure modes: (1) prospects who never accept the connection request (email pivot needed), and (2) prospects who accepted but never replied (free DM follow-up until cold). As of April 2, six active prospects are in the email-pivot track (all awaiting JT send), while Henick-Lane is in the free DM track at M4 (the final touch). The batch that sent M1 on 2026-03-23/24 has now cycled fully to its terminal state in under 10 days — meaning the effective outreach window per LinkedIn batch is about 10-12 days before you're either in reply or cold. Building the next batch (G-Net, Maxwell Plumb, Superior Plumbing, G-Net) while the prior batch reaches cold is the only way to maintain pipeline velocity without waiting for lagging replies. (Source: pipeline review 2026-04-02)

[2026-03-31] CONSTRUCTION — Plumbing violation removal is unique among construction services because it has a hard, externally-enforced deadline with financial penalties that accrue daily after the certification window closes (HPD Class A violations: $50-150/day after deadline). The job tracker pitch for violation removal contractors is different from general GC: it's not about speed or efficiency, it's about deadline visibility — specifically, preventing a removal job from slipping through the cracks while the crew is responding to an emergency. The hook is the consequence, not the inconvenience.

[2026-03-30] CONSTRUCTION — Multi-sector GCs (hospitals + schools + tenant improvements + facility maintenance + emergency restoration) have a fundamentally different tracking problem than single-sector contractors: each client type brings its own compliance cadence, reporting format, and inspection schedule. G-Net Construction runs 7 service lines simultaneously with 65 years of embedded manual process. The architect/principal (Vincent Nativo, RA/AIA/LEED-AP) personally manages permit review — which means the coordination overhead from mixed project types lands directly on the most credentialed person in the firm. Automating job classification by type and compliance cadence is the pitch: not "your tracking is slow" but "your tracking treats hospital jobs and school jobs the same." (Source: G-Net Construction Corp research 2026-03-30)

[2026-03-29] PROPERTY MANAGEMENT — Tech-forward PM firms that explicitly market "real-time vendor coordination" and "seamless coordination between residents, staff, and vendors" (HPM's exact Yelp language) are the easiest AI pitch: they've already articulated the value, the gap is between the aspiration and what the portal actually does. HPM markets a mobile app + online portal + real-time project tracking, but the vendor routing and status update layer after intake is still manual. The brand promise creates the opening — the pitch doesn't have to sell the concept, only the delivery mechanism. This pattern holds across three PM firms now: HPM, MyPropertyMan ("streamline to-do lists"), Atlas NYC ("proactive management"). (Source: HPM Harlem Property Management research 2026-03-29)

[2026-03-28] CONSTRUCTION/TRADES — Dual-trade contractors (plumbing + HVAC, or HVAC + fire safety) have a more complex dispatch problem than single-trade shops: every job potentially involves two separate trade crews, two parts catalogs, and two scheduling queues. Maxwell Plumb ($16.4M, 30 emp, Bayside Queens) runs commercial plumbing + HVAC + fire safety 24/7 across all 5 boroughs with a live-operator dispatch model. The pitch to a dual-trade shop is fundamentally different from single-trade HVAC — the automation layer has to separate emergency from scheduled AND route by trade type simultaneously. This is a more specific pain than "dispatch is slow" and maps directly to the construction-job-tracker template's priority + trade routing logic. (Source: Maxwell Plumb Mechanical Corp research 2026-03-28)
