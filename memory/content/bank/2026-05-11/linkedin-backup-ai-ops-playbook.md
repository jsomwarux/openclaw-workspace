An AI ops playbook is useful if it changes the next decision.

A list of tools does not matter. A list of workflows does not matter. The useful artifact tells you what to inspect, what to pause, who owns the next step, and what proof would make the build safe.

That is why I keep coming back to diagnostics before automation.

The buyer does not need a prettier map of the process.

They need a short path from “this is broken” to “this is the first workflow worth building.”
