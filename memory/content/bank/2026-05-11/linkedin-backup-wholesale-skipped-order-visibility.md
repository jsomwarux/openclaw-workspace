Wholesale distributors say customers can get what they need through the catalog, but the order is lost when an availability check, quote, or replenishment cycle stalls without an owner.

The pattern shows up most in route-based and parts-heavy businesses: the customer does not churn because the product disappeared. They churn because nobody noticed the normal buying rhythm broke.

They need skipped-order visibility before they need a smarter catalog.

One place that flags aging quotes, missed replenishment windows, delayed delivery promises, and high-value customers who stopped following their normal pattern.
