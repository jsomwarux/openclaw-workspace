Nine out of ten organizations have deployed AI tools.

Nine out of ten haven't gotten past the first pilot. The research number: 90% deployment, 9% maturity. That gap exists in the implementation and adoption layer, not the technology layer.

What this means for how the market is moving: the demand is not for AI builders. Builders are cheaper and faster than ever to hire. The demand is for practitioners who own what happens after the tool is live. Workflow redesign. Team adoption. Performance monitoring. The handoff between a working demo and a working operation.

That's a different skill set from writing prompts or connecting APIs. The organizations bridging the gap are not hiring more engineers. They're hiring people who've run real implementations and understand why they fail.
