# Wednesday LinkedIn Case Study — 2026-04-08 (draft for JT review)

**Angle:** Construction compliance deadline tracking — the invisible cost

---

A contractor finds out a job passed its HPD inspection window.

Two months of work, and the final sign-off never came because nobody tracked the closeout deadline.

This happens more than you'd think.

Different job types carry different compliance requirements:

— Hospital renovation: DOH inspection window closes 30 days after substantial completion
— School work: NYC DOE/SCA sign-off required before state reimbursement kicks in  
— Class A plumbing: HPD violation accrues $150/day after the certification window closes
— Flood restoration: DEP + DOB both have independent deadlines, easily missed in sequence

The problem isn't complexity. It's that the institutional knowledge lives in the foreman's head — what needs to happen at closeout, which agency, when.

When the foreman moves to the next job, that context moves with them.

I built a workflow around this:

When a foreman marks a job complete via WhatsApp, the system automatically pulls the compliance requirements for that job type and alerts the owner before any window closes.

No more chasing the previous crew. No more compliance surprises. The owner has a complete record of every completed job, organized by deadline.

One missed HPD inspection window: $150/day in accruing fines. This system costs less than that first penalty.

This is what I mean when I say AI automation isn't about replacing judgment — it's about removing the coordination overhead that makes costly mistakes inevitable.
