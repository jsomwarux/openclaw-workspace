Every NYC contractor I spoke to during outreach had the same problem.

The foreman finishes a job. The owner needs to update the client. So the owner calls the foreman. The foreman is on the next site. Three calls later, somebody gets a voicemail.

That cycle was eating 30+ minutes a day — for a task that adds zero value to anyone.

So I built a workflow around the pattern:

The foreman sends one WhatsApp message when a job wraps. Claude reads it, classifies the status, and a professional client update goes out automatically. If there's a blocker — missing materials, weather delay, permit issue — the owner gets flagged. If everything's clean, nothing else happens.

The foreman changed nothing about how he works. The owner stopped playing phone tag. The client started getting same-day updates.

The workflow took a few hours to configure. The time it saves compounds every single day.

This is what I mean when I say AI automation isn't about replacing your crew — it's about removing the coordination tax your crew doesn't even know they're paying.
