## Draft
A major CRM platform just paid $3.6B for a customer-service agent company.

The useful signal sits in run control. The queue owner matters. So does the exception path, escalation rule, log, and team that can trust the output.

The AI agent market is moving toward the workflow after the model reads the request.

That is the implementation layer most teams skip in the demo. The first version handles clean cases. The real system needs a review queue for uncertain cases, an approval path for sensitive actions, and a log that shows what happened after the handoff.

I see that pattern in practice. Teams do not get stuck because they cannot pick a model. They get stuck because nobody has named the exception owner, approval rule, or final record.

Access to capable AI is table stakes now. The operating layer around it justifies the budget.
