## Native pattern teardown

1. Opening syntax: direct threshold questions work best. “How many rookie RBs over X?” “Is he locked in as a 2nd rounder?” “When’s the right time?” These invite replies without sounding like engagement bait.
2. Native nouns: rookie RB, rookie draft, RB56, RB84, KTC, FantasyCalc, TE24, QB35, 2027 early 1st, card, slip, leg, +689, MLB/NBA, round robin, last leg.
3. Rhythm: short line breaks, one decision per line, no setup paragraph. Dynasty posts should feel like market-room talk. Betting posts should feel like a card review.
4. Tension type: player tier disagreement, price gap vs role case, timing after rookie hype, last-leg excitement leak, round-robin false safety.
5. What NOT to copy: author persona, giveaway/promo framing, emoji bait, “drop at 10 likes,” app promo, raw ranking dumps, or any exact swipe wording.

Quality gate applied: Each draft maps to a native syntax/rhythm mechanic above. At least 3 drafts use current player/market/card specificity. Product references appear only in the internal angle notes.
