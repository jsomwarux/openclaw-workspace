The scariest automation failure is the one that looks like success.

Your n8n workflow ran for three months. Zero errors. Green checkmarks every time.

You open the sheet. Nothing added in 6 weeks.

I built a StreetEasy scraper that worked exactly this way. It looked perfect. The sheet went dark. The client never noticed.

The fix was not a more complex workflow. It was a daily Telegram ping that shows you what the scraper actually pulled that morning.

Automation that does not tell you when it breaks is not automation. It is a quiet disaster waiting to cost you something you will not notice until it matters.
