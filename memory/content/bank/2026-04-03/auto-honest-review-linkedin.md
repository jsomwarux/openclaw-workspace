## Draft

I've run an AI agent 24/7 for 8 months. Here's what that's actually like.

Month 1-3: task execution. It did what I asked. Useful. Not transformative.

Month 4-5: cron scheduling kicked in. The agent started running without being asked. That's when the real time savings started.

Now: 20+ scheduled jobs, daily briefs, content generation, job market monitoring, client research pipelines. Most of it runs while I sleep.

The honest verdict isn't about the AI. It's about what you design it to care about.

The automations that compound aren't the clever ones. They're the boring ones that run on a schedule and touch something you used to do every single day.

That's what 8 months taught me. The technology was ready. The work was figuring out what to point it at.
