NYC contractors spend 30 to 60 minutes every day calling their foremen for job status.

I built a workflow that replaces those calls. The foreman sends one WhatsApp message at the end of the shift, the same thing they'd normally say on the phone. Claude reads it, classifies the job as on-track, at-risk, or blocked, and routes accordingly. Client gets a professional update. Job gets logged. Owner gets alerted only when something is blocking progress.

Built this as a reusable template for NYC contractors. Configurable for a new client in about two hours.

No app to download. No new system for the crew to learn. Just a message in a channel they already use.

The design decision that mattered most: most owners don't need confirmation a job is running fine. They need to know the instant it isn't. Automating the warning is worth more than automating the status report.

The crew doesn't change anything. The signal does.
