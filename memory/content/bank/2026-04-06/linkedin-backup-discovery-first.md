The question that changes every build: what are you trying to prevent?

Not "what should the workflow do." What happens in the operation when it fails.

For the construction job tracker, the ask was status updates. The actual problem was that blocked jobs weren't surfacing until the owner called the foreman.

The workflow design changed when that distinction was clear.
