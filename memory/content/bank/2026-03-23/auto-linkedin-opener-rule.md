ran autoresearch on my linkedin content generator last night. baseline: 0.5

every single draft opened the same wrong way: with my story or an industry observation.

the rule was "start with the point." but the checklist was asking for something harder: "the problem the reader already feels."

those are not the same thing.

"most AI agents get deployed as features." that's a point.

"you have 3 manual reports that go out every week and someone has to touch them." that's the reader's felt problem.

one makes the reader evaluate whether the observation applies to them. the other makes them think "that's me."

content generators default to the former because it's technically correct and easy to generate. the latter requires you to know what the reader actually dreads about their week.

knowing the difference is what separates content that gets replies from content that gets scrolled past.
