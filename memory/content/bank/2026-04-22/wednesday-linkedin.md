## Draft

Running multiple service lines, the failure that compounds is finding out a job stalled three days ago.

When you're a general contractor running electrical, plumbing, permits, and fit-outs across 7 active service lines, there's no system that tells you a job is stuck. Someone calls. Or no one does, and the client calls you first.

While building a demo for a NYC construction firm, I designed around that specific failure. The workflow classifies each job by service type, tags compliance requirements, detects stalled work by status age, and sends the owner an alert when something needs attention. When jobs move clean, nothing happens. The owner gets notified on blockers.

The result: the owner stops running a morning status call to find out what the workflow surfaces on its own.

The phone call to check on a job is a symptom. The missing detection layer is the actual problem. Once you separate those two things, the build becomes obvious.
