A tenant email, a delinquency report, a maintenance note, and an owner approval rule should not become four separate follow-up systems.

That is usually where property-management AI gets messy.

Before I build one of these workflows, I want the boring parts clear:

Can you name the intake source?
Can you name the system of record?
Can you name the approval owner?
Can you name the exception path?
Can you show enough volume or pain to justify the build?

If those answers are clear, the workflow can stay narrow and useful.

It can read the report, classify the row, draft the clean follow-up, and route the sensitive case to a manager before anything leaves the business.

If those answers are not clear, AI just makes the broken handoff faster.

I put together a readiness checklist for property managers who want to pressure-test a rent follow-up, maintenance, missed-call, or owner-approval workflow before building around it.

Happy to share it.
