## LinkedIn Draft

AI automation has a boring failure mode:

Teams automate the visible task before they understand the broken handoff.

That is why so many AI demos look impressive but never become operationally useful.

The value is usually not in the chatbot, scraper, or dashboard by itself.

It is in finding the point where work changes hands and context disappears.

A tenant request becomes a vendor follow-up.
A customer question becomes a back-office lookup.
A sales inquiry becomes a spreadsheet update.
A manager’s “quick check” becomes three systems and two Slack messages.

That is where automation actually pays.

Not because AI is magical.

Because the business already has the demand, the data, and the pain. It just has no clean path between them.

The strongest implementation work starts with operations mapping, not model selection.

If the handoff is messy, the automation will be messy.

Fix the workflow first. Then add AI.
