A rent delinquency report does not need a thinking agent.

It needs a safer operating path.

The report already has most of the facts:

- tenant
- unit
- balance
- last payment
- payment-plan status
- manager notes
- legal or owner-sensitive flags

The risky part is what happens next.

Before any reminder goes out, the workflow should show the source row, the missing context, the suggested next step, and the reviewer who can approve, edit, hold, or escalate it.

That is the layer I am building for property-management work.

The useful proof is a control record:

- what the system read
- what it drafted
- what it blocked
- who reviewed it
- what evidence gets logged before anything tenant-sensitive moves

A lot of property-management "AI" should probably be boring automation.

Facts. But the business still needs the control surface around it.

Automation can move the clean work.

The human review queue protects the sensitive work.
