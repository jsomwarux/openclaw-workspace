## Avallon

### What They Do

**One-liner:** AI agents that automate insurance claims operations end-to-end — from intake calls to document processing to data entry — so adjusters can resolve cases faster.

**Details:**
- HQ: New York City (rare advantage — same market as JT)
- Stage: Seed ($4.6M, November 2025), led by Frontline Ventures; also backed by Y Combinator (Spring 2025 cohort), 1984, Liquid2, Booom
- Core product: Multimodal AI agents that handle the *full* claims workflow — inbound phone calls, emails, document triage, data extraction from medical reports, coordination with employers and repair shops, and data entry into CMS platforms. Not a point solution; they call it a "digital teammate."
- Integration: Connects directly with existing CMS platforms (claims management systems), IVR systems, and data warehouses — zero disruption to current stacks
- Initial verticals: Workers Compensation and Automotive insurance; expanding to all P&C lines + healthcare with seed capital
- **Target customers: Third-party administrators (TPAs), MGAs, and insurance carriers.** They are not selling to individual agencies — they sell to the operational layer that *processes* claims at scale.

**Traction (as of Nov 2025):**
- 10x revenue growth since completing YC Spring 2025 cohort
- Contracts live in US and Europe
- Named client: Athens Administrators (California-based multiline TPA) — 64% of inbound calls automatically resolved
- "Nationwide partner with more than 400 adjusters" referenced (unnamed)
- Industry context they're riding: BLS projects 400,000 insurance workers lost to attrition by 2026

**Founding team:**
- Cornelius Schramm (CEO) — Cornell/FINN (European fleet ops, fleet claims processes)
- Bryan Guin (COO) — Cornell
- Moritz Bartusch — MIT ML researcher; previously built AI decision engines at Taktile for fintechs and insurers including Allianz
- Leander Peter — Core infra engineering, FINN Europe

## FurtherAI

### What They Do

**One-liner:** AI workspace purpose-built for commercial insurance — automates document processing, submissions intake, underwriting audits, policy comparisons, and claims workflows for large insurers and MGAs.

**Details:**
- HQ: San Francisco, CA
- Stage: Series A — $25M led by Andreessen Horowitz (October 2025), plus prior $5M seed round; total funding ~$30M (Tracxn cites $35.6M, possibly includes additional rounds)
- Founded: 2023 by Aman Gour and Sashank Gondala
- Also backed by: Nexus Venture Partners, Y Combinator, Xceedance, BTV (insurance-focused funds)
- Core product: An "AI Workspace" — not a single-use tool but a multi-workflow platform. Automates: submission intake, underwriting audits, policy/coverage comparison, proposal generation, claims intake, compliance/QA, and portfolio analytics
- Key differentiator: "Forward-deployed engineering" model — FurtherAI's engineers embed with the customer's team to build and stand up workflows. This is hands-on, white-glove, and means FurtherAI is currently doing its own implementation.
- Target customers: Commercial insurers — large MGAs, specialty carriers, risk exchanges, regional mutuals, and fast-growing insurtechs. Named clients include Accelerant (largest MGA in US, >$1.5B premiums), MSI, Leavitt Group, and a top-10 global carrier with >$20B GWP.

**Traction (as of Oct 2025):**
- Processing billions in premiums annually
- 95% data accuracy; 30x faster quote readiness; 646% ROI claimed
- 15% improvement in submission-to-quote ratios for clients
- 10x proposal generation speed
- A16Z called it "one of the largest Series A ever raised in insurance AI" — that's meaningful signal on trajectory

**Founding team:**
- Aman Gour — CEO & Co-Founder
- Sashank Gondala — CTO & Co-Founder
- Ben Grosser — Insurance veteran; built and scaled underwriting models for top carriers (mentioned in earlier funding announcement)

## Strategic Recommendation

**Avallon is the clear first move.** Three reasons: (1) They're NYC-based and seed-stage — founders are accessible and the company has zero sales infrastructure overhead. Cornelius Schramm will respond to a well-crafted LinkedIn DM or email personally. (2) Their target buyers (TPAs, mid-market MGAs) are exactly the profile of insurance operations clients in JT's NYC niche. The match between Avallon's product and JT Somwaru Consulting's target client base is tighter than FurtherAI's. (3) At seed stage with 10x revenue growth, they need distribution they can't build fast enough themselves — JT Somwaru Consulting is exactly the kind of partner they should want. There's no formal program to navigate; just a founder conversation.

**FurtherAI is a 6-12 month play.** They're moving fast ($30M raised, a16z-backed), but their current motion is enterprise direct. JT should plant a flag now — book a demo, get on their radar, ask openly about implementation partnerships — but don't expect a formal program to engage with until they've professionalized their GTM. Monitor their jobs page for partnerships hires; that's the trigger signal. If Avallon leads to any NYC insurance agency wins, use those as proof-of-concept when pitching FurtherAI: "I've already deployed insurance AI for [X] and [Y] in NYC — let's talk about formalizing this."
