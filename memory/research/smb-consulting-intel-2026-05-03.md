# SMB Consulting Intel — 2026-05-03

## Focus
Sunday heartbeat proactive research pass: market signals for JT's consulting wedge in SMB property management and wholesale distribution.

## Signal 1 — Property managers still want maintenance triage tied to renewal economics
Recent search results surfaced multiple property-management automation posts around AI maintenance triage, dispatch, tenant follow-up, and renewal impact. The useful angle is not “AI chatbot”; it is operational leakage: unresolved maintenance, slow vendor dispatch, and missing follow-ups show up as tenant churn.

**Consulting wedge:** 7-day maintenance response audit:
- ingest last 60–90 days of maintenance tickets / emails / forms
- classify by type, urgency, vendor, response time, and unresolved follow-up
- identify the top 3 repeat failure loops
- deliver a lightweight triage + follow-up workflow before pitching a bigger dashboard

**Outreach hook:** “Most small PM teams do not need another portal. They need to know which maintenance requests quietly cost renewals.”

## Signal 2 — Wholesale AI conversation is still inventory + forecasting, but SMB gap is messy handoff work
Recent distribution/wholesale AI results emphasized inventory, forecasting, order automation, and CRM/ERP modernization. For SMB distributors, the practical gap is still between inbox/spreadsheets and the system of record: quote intake, reorder timing, stockout flags, and vendor/customer follow-up.

**Consulting wedge:** operational audit for quote-to-reorder leakage:
- map inbound quote/order channels
- identify manual re-entry points
- flag SKUs/customers with recurring stockout or delay patterns
- automate one small workflow: quote intake routing, low-stock exception list, or reorder follow-up

**Outreach hook:** “If your team is still catching stockout risk from inbox context, the first AI win is not prediction — it is exception visibility.”

## Why this matters for JT
Both niches support the Services-as-Software model: start with a narrow audit, document the Client OS, ship one operational workflow, then productize repeat patterns across similar SMBs.
