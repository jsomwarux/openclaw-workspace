# SMB Consulting Intel — 2026-05-04

## Focus
Heartbeat proactive research pass: current signals for JT's Services-as-Software consulting wedge across property management, construction, and wholesale SMBs.

## Signal 1 — Maintenance triage keeps showing up as the clearest property-management wedge
Fresh search results again surfaced AI maintenance triage/dispatch for SMB property management. The pattern is consistent: small teams do not need a generic chatbot; they need faster classification, vendor routing, tenant follow-up, and visibility into unresolved requests.

**Offer angle:** 7-day maintenance leakage audit.
- Pull last 60–90 days of maintenance emails/forms/tickets.
- Classify by urgency, category, vendor, response time, and unresolved follow-up.
- Deliver a simple exception dashboard plus one automated follow-up/routing flow.

**Why it sells:** Tie the pain to renewals, bad reviews, owner complaints, and staff time — not “AI.”

## Signal 2 — SMB AI adoption content is emphasizing cost reduction, but JT should sell exception visibility
Recent small-business AI workflow pieces keep leading with broad efficiency/cost-reduction claims. That positioning is crowded. For JT, the sharper wedge is: “your team is doing the work, but management cannot see the exceptions until they become expensive.”

**Reusable phrase:** AI is not the product. Exception visibility is the product.

## Signal 3 — Wholesale/distribution opportunity remains quote/order/reorder handoff leakage
The most practical SMB distribution angle is not advanced forecasting first. It is cleaning up the messy middle: quote intake, SKU/customer exceptions, delayed reorders, low-stock warnings, and email-to-ERP handoffs.

**Offer angle:** quote-to-reorder leakage audit.
- Map inbound quote/order channels.
- Identify manual re-entry and missed follow-up points.
- Produce one exception list or workflow that catches revenue leakage earlier.

## Recommended next concrete move
Create one tiny demo screen for the portfolio/client pitch: “Exception Dashboard for SMB Operators” with three tabs:
1. Maintenance requests aging without vendor assignment.

This unifies the consulting story across niches without sounding generic.
