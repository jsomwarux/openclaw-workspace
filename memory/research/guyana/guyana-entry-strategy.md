# Guyana Entry Strategy — JT Somwaru Consulting
**Created:** March 15, 2026

---

## The Situation

JT has a personal contact who can potentially connect him with someone in Guyana (local firm or government connection). This is the single most important asset for entering this market — Guyana runs on relationships, not cold pitches.

The opportunity is real: fastest-growing economy in the world, government actively spending on AI and digital transformation, drainage/infrastructure accountability gaps that directly match JT's capabilities.

---

## Phase 1: Validate the Contact (Do This First)

Before building anything or researching further, get answers to these questions from JT's contact:

1. **Who can they connect JT with?** Government ministry official, local consulting firm, or private sector operator?
2. **Is the connection active now?** Can an intro happen in the next 2–4 weeks?
3. **What's the contact's relationship with that person?** Personal (strong) or professional acquaintance (weaker)?
4. **Is the contact Guyanese or US-based?** This determines whether they can broker in-person meetings

The answer shapes the entire strategy. A direct ministry intro is a 6-month path to a contract. A cold firm intro is an 18-month path.

---

## Phase 2: Build Demo (Parallel with Phase 1)

While validating the contact, build **Georgetown City Services Agent** (Build 1 from demo-build-plan.md).

Timeline: 1–2 days.

This is the only demo needed for an initial meeting. It uses real Georgetown context, directly references the government's December 2025 drainage/digitization commitment, and shows a complete working system — not slides.

---

## Phase 3: Identify Local Partner Candidates

Guyana's procurement system heavily favors local businesses. For any government contract, JT either needs:

**Option A: Local technology firm as prime contractor** — JT comes in as the AI/automation specialist they sub-contract. They handle the relationship and procurement; JT delivers the technical work.

**Option B: Local general consulting firm** — Same structure. They win the engagement; JT is the AI layer.

**Option C: Register a Guyanese entity** — More complex, 6–12 months, but enables JT to bid directly. Only worth exploring if initial relationships develop.

### Firms to research (starting point):
- Search "technology consulting firm Georgetown Guyana" and "IT services company Guyana government"
- Look for firms that have already won NPTA contracts (public data at npta.gov.gy)
- US Trade.gov Guyana commercial guide has a local partner directory

---

## Phase 4: Georgetown Trip

Government consulting in Guyana **requires in-person presence** for initial engagement. A trip to Georgetown is non-negotiable if this moves forward.

**Trip purpose:**
- Meet the intro contact in person
- Demo the City Services Agent live
- Identify 2–3 local firm partner candidates
- Visit NPTA or relevant ministry offices if intro allows

**Logistics:**
- Direct flights: New York (JFK) → Georgetown (GEO/Cheddi Jagan International) — ~5 hours
- US citizens can enter Guyana visa-free for tourism/business (up to 30 days)
- Georgetown hotels: Marriott Georgetown is the business-class option; US firms and contractors stay there

## Pricing Framework for Guyana

Government contracts are larger than JT's current NYC SMB pricing but require more scope definition. Starting framework:

| Engagement | Scope | Price Range |
|---|---|---|
| City Services Agent (pilot) | Single city, 3-month pilot, 2 service categories | $15,000–$25,000 |
| Full municipal digitization | 5+ service categories, dashboard, 12-month support | $75,000–$150,000 |
| Infrastructure monitoring dashboard | 10–20 active projects, ministry briefings, 12-month | $50,000–$100,000 |
| Procurement audit system | NPTA integration, weekly reports, ministry access, 12-month | $40,000–$80,000 |
| Full suite (all three) | All of the above, 24-month engagement | $200,000–$400,000 |

**Important:** Price in USD. Payment milestones (30% upfront, 30% mid-delivery, 40% on completion) are standard for government consulting to manage 90–180 day payment cycle risk.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Procurement awarded through connections | Local partner as prime; JT as sub-contractor |
| Slow government payment | Milestone-based contracts, 30% upfront |
| Corruption pressure | Operate through US entity, document everything, no cash arrangements |
| Travel required | Budget 1–2 trips to Georgetown to close initial engagement |
| Competition from large firms | JT's advantage: speed, personalization, AI-native vs. traditional IT firms |

---

## Decision Trigger

**Go signal:** Contact confirms a direct intro to ministry official or local firm with active government work. Then build demo + plan trip.

**Hold signal:** Contact's connection is loose/informal, or is private sector only with no government line. Research further before investing build time.

**Stop signal:** Contact cannot make a meaningful intro. Park this in future-signals.md and revisit if another Guyana connection surfaces.
