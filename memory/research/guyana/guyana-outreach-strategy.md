## The Core Principle

Guyana runs on relationships. Cold email to a ministry inbox goes nowhere. The fastest path now is private-sector supplier/operator conversations plus local firm partners. Use diaspora/family/AmCham/GCCI channels to create warm intros; use government channels only when they lead to a specific buyer or local partner.

---

## TIER 1 — Government Entry (Without a Contact)

Government ministries don't respond to cold outreach from foreign consultants. But there are three legitimate channels that can get you in front of the right people: GO-INVEST (their job is to introduce foreign companies to government), AmCham Guyana (US business bridge), and the US Embassy Commercial Section (advocacy for American companies).

### 1A. GO-INVEST (Guyana Office for Investment)

**What it is:** Semi-autonomous government agency whose literal job is to introduce foreign investors to the right people in Guyana. They will take your call.

**Why it works for you:** They have direct relationships with ministry officials, can facilitate introductions to NDMA (e-government) and MLGRD (local government/drainage), and have advocated for US tech companies before.

**Contact:**
- Website: guyanainvest.gov.gy
- Email: invest@goinvest.gov.gy (primary inquiry)
- Physical: 190 Camp Street, Georgetown, Guyana
- LinkedIn: search "GO-INVEST Guyana" — they have an active presence

**Approach:**
Email them positioning yourself as a US-based AI automation consultant (Guyanese-American) interested in exploring digital transformation opportunities aligned with Guyana's ICT Masterplan 2030 and the Digital Guyana initiative. Ask for a 20-minute intro call to understand which ministries are actively seeking technology partners. This is a warm channel — they want to facilitate this.

**What to say:** "I'm a Guyanese-American AI automation consultant based in New York. I've been following Guyana's Digital Guyana initiative and believe there's a direct fit between the systems I build — particularly for city services automation and government workflow modernization — and the government's stated priorities. I'd like to explore how GO-INVEST can help facilitate introductions to the right ministry contacts."

---

### 1B. National Data Management Authority (NDMA)

**What it is:** The government agency responsible for designing, developing, and deploying eServices across all government ministries. They built the Citizen Portal, AskGov, and are the operational layer for the Digital Guyana initiative.

**Why it's the right target:** NDMA is the technical buyer, not just a policy ministry. They evaluate and procure AI/automation tools. If you're pitching city services automation or workflow AI, NDMA is the implementation partner.

**Confirmed contact:**
- Lloyd Marks — Manager, eServices at NDMA
  - LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/lloyd-marks-8a0a2b43
  - This is a confirmed LinkedIn profile surfaced in search results
- NDMA main contact: ndma.gov.gy | support.ndma.gov.gy
- Facebook (active): facebook.com/NDMAGuyana (7,425 followers)

**Approach:**
LinkedIn DM to Lloyd Marks. Keep it short. Frame it as a capability introduction, not a pitch. Example: "Lloyd — I'm a US-based AI automation consultant (Guyanese-American, based in NYC). I've been watching NDMA's progress on the Citizen Portal and AskGov with a lot of interest. I build workflow automation systems on n8n and Agentforce that complement exactly the kind of eServices infrastructure you're building — particularly for complaint routing and multi-agency coordination. Worth a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit?"

### 1C. Ministry of Local Government & Regional Development (MLGRD)

**What it is:** The ministry responsible for Georgetown's infrastructure, the City Council, drainage, and the Georgetown City Revival Plan that President Ali personally commissioned.

**Why it's the right target:** The drainage crisis, waste management, and city services digitization are all under MLGRD's mandate. This is the ministry that needs your Georgetown City Services Agent most directly.

**Contact:**
- Address: De Winkle Building, Fort Street, Kingston, Georgetown
- Website: mlgrd.gov.gy (currently under maintenance as of March 2026)
- Facebook (active): facebook.com/LocalGovGuyana
- Email pattern for Guyana government: typically [firstname.lastname]@mlgrd.gov.gy

**Approach:**
Don't cold-email this ministry directly. Instead, reach them through GO-INVEST (1A above) or through a local firm introduction (Tier 2). Once you have a local partner, they approach the ministry jointly — foreign consultant + local firm as a team changes the dynamic entirely. If you must go direct, a letter addressed to the Permanent Secretary (not the Minister) with a one-page capability overview is more likely to land than email.

### 1E. US Embassy Commercial Section (Georgetown)

**What it is:** The US Embassy has a dedicated Commercial Section that helps US companies enter the Guyanese market. They provide market intelligence, facilitate introductions to government officials, and advocate for US firms in procurement processes.

**Why it matters:** As a US-based company, you have a right to request advocacy support. They actively work to open doors for American firms.

**Contact:**
- Trade.gov: trade.gov/guyana-contact-us
- Commercial Section: US Embassy, Young & Duke Streets, Georgetown
- Phone: (529) 225-4900
- Deputy Chief of Mission: John Crippen (as of August 2025)
- Embassy Facebook: facebook.com/USEmbassyGeorgetown

**Approach:**
Submit a Market Access inquiry through trade.gov. Describe your services, identify the specific Guyana government programs you're targeting (Digital Guyana, NDMA eServices, MLGRD drainage modernization), and ask whether the Commercial Section can facilitate introductions. This is a free service for US companies.

## TIER 3 — Diaspora Network (NYC Channels)

This is JT's unique asymmetric advantage. No generic consultant from the US can walk into Guyanese community spaces the way you can as a Guyanese-American. These channels are warmer than any cold LinkedIn message to Georgetown.

**Why it's #1:** The Consulate's explicit mandate is diaspora economic engagement. Consul General Michael Brotherson hosted a government-sponsored job fair in Queens in July 2024 specifically to connect diaspora professionals with Guyana business opportunities. This is exactly the channel designed for JT's situation.

**Why it matters:** Less business-focused than the Consulate, but cultural events are where you find Guyanese business owners, community leaders, and professionals who have family/business connections back home. In Guyana's relationship-driven culture, a recommendation from a mutual community connection is worth more than any LinkedIn message.

**Contact:**
