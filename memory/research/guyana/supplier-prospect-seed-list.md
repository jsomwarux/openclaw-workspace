## 2026 Local Content Annual Plan approval qualification — 2026-06-29

Source signal:
- Kaieteur News reported that the Ministry of Natural Resources' Local Content Secretariat signed over 40 Local Content Annual Plans for the 2026 reporting year on 2026-06-17/18. Named examples included ExxonMobil Guyana Ltd., MODEC Guyana Inc., CNOOC Petroleum Guyana Ltd., Guyana Deep Water Operations Inc., SLB, Halliburton, Saipem, Baker Hughes, Ramps Logistics, Technip, Airswift, and G-Port.
- INews Guyana/DPI separately reported Minister Vickram Bharrat's language about closing "rent-a-citizen" and shell-company loopholes and pushing for real Guyanese participation.
- URLs: https://kaieteurnewsonline.com/2026/06/18/govt-approves-over-40-local-content-annual-plans-for-2026/ ; https://inewsguyana.com/local-content-loopholes-closed-opportunities-boosted-for-guyanese-bharrat/

Qualification verdict:
- No confirmed direct overlap with the prior first-five T2 supplier outreach batch: Excel Guyana, GLASS, IGOT Guyana, TLC Guyana, and AquaTerra Marine were not named in the public article excerpt.
- The named approval-plan companies are mostly operators, global oilfield contractors, and large service providers. Treat them as buyer/channel or ecosystem-validation targets, not immediate cold-outreach prospects.
- This is still commercially relevant because approved annual plans include procurement, employment, and capacity-development sub-plans, and half-yearly reporting follows the approved plan. That maps to JT's Local Content Ops Sprint only when a named operator, supplier, procurement owner, or local partner confirms workflow pain.
