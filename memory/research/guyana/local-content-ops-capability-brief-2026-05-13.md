## One-page capability brief copy

### Local Content Operations Sprint

Guyana’s oil and gas growth is creating real opportunity for local suppliers, but it also creates a heavier admin layer: certificates, renewals, supplier profiles, bid deadlines, vendor documents, quote follow-ups, compliance evidence, and customer requests spread across inboxes, WhatsApp, folders, and spreadsheets.

The Local Content Operations Sprint helps Guyana-connected suppliers clean up that operating layer before it costs them deadlines, contracts, or credibility with larger customers.

This is not a broad AI transformation project. It is a practical 2–3 week workflow sprint focused on the supplier work that actually gets stuck.

## Buyer pain

This is for operators, supplier owners, compliance/admin leads, procurement teams, logistics firms, staffing providers, marine support firms, safety vendors, and service contractors who are dealing with questions like:

- Which certification or renewal is due next?
- Which bid/tender has missing documents?
- Who owns the follow-up with the customer, vendor, or prime contractor?
- Where is the latest insurance, safety, tax, NIS/GRA, or capability document?
- What opportunities are aging without action?
- What is stuck in WhatsApp or someone’s inbox?
- What does the owner need to approve this week?

## What the sprint includes

1. **Workflow map** — current process from request/intake to document collection, approval, bid submission, and follow-up.
2. **Compliance/document checklist** — required documents, owners, due dates, renewal dates, and evidence links.
3. **Tender/bid tracker** — opportunities, deadlines, status, owner, document pack readiness, and next actions.
4. **Vendor/customer CRM lite** — key contacts, category, last touch, next action, and open items.
5. **Document intake setup** — folder structure, naming convention, intake form, and missing-document process.
6. **Exception dashboard** — overdue renewals, missing docs, aging bids, unassigned tasks, pending approvals, and upcoming deadlines.
7. **Automation where useful** — reminders, owner summaries, status updates, handoff alerts, and dashboard refreshes.
8. **Runbook** — simple instructions so the team can keep using the system after the sprint.

## Example workflows

### 1. Certification and renewal tracker
Track local-content certificate status, renewal dates, missing supporting documents, owner, and evidence links in one place.

### 2. Tender/bid readiness desk
Show every active opportunity, deadline, required documents, pack completeness, owner, next action, and risk flag.

### 3. Supplier document intake
Use a standard intake form and folder structure so documents do not get lost in email or WhatsApp.

### 4. Vendor/customer follow-up board
Track customer requests, vendor documents, quote follow-ups, invoice follow-ups, and next actions.

### 5. Owner exception dashboard
A weekly view of what changed, what is stuck, who owns it, and what needs approval.
