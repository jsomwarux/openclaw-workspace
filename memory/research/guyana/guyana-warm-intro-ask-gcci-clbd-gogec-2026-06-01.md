## Operating posture

Use this when the ask is warm, routed, or connector-led. Do not use it as a mass message.

JT is asking to learn from private-sector operators, not to access government contracts or pitch broad technology change. The right intro target is someone close to documents, renewals, bid packs, quote follow-up, supplier registration, compliance evidence, logistics/service records, or owner visibility.

## Best intro targets

- Local-content suppliers and support-service firms.
- Logistics, customs, marine support, offshore service, staffing/manpower, safety/compliance, procurement, and equipment/service contractors.
- Chamber or ecosystem operators who can point to members dealing with supplier readiness and admin follow-up.
- Local tech, accounting, consulting, or compliance partners already serving these firms.

## Path 1 - GCCI / chamber connector ask

Best for: GCCI, AmCham, WCCIG, local chamber members, or anyone who can route JT to growing suppliers.

Draft:

Hi [Name],

I am looking for 1-2 operator conversations around a narrow local-content workflow problem in Guyana.

Not a broad pitch and not a request for government access. I am trying to learn where suppliers and support-service firms are getting stuck with the admin layer: bid packs, supplier documents, renewals, quote follow-up, compliance evidence, customer/vendor updates, and owner approvals.

The best person would be someone at a logistics, marine support, staffing, safety/compliance, procurement, equipment, or oil/gas support-service company who sees that work every week.

Would anyone in the GCCI / chamber network be worth learning from?

## Path 2 - CLBD / supplier-development ask

Best for: CLBD-style supplier development, local-content readiness, training, procurement-readiness, or business-support contacts.

Draft:

Hi [Name],

I am trying to understand one practical problem for Guyana-connected suppliers: how the admin work around local-content readiness actually gets managed day to day.

The workflows I am looking at are specific: certification and renewal tracking, bid/tender readiness, supplier document collection, compliance evidence, customer/vendor follow-up, and simple visibility into what is stuck.

I am not looking for procurement access or trying to sell a big system. I am trying to validate whether a small workflow audit would be useful for operators who are growing into larger opportunities.

Is there a supplier, admin lead, compliance lead, or advisor you think would give a grounded view?

## Path 3 - GOGEC / oil-and-gas ecosystem ask

Best for: oil/gas support-service connectors, operator networks, local partner channels, or people close to offshore-services suppliers.

Draft:

Hi [Name],

I am researching a narrow operating problem in Guyana's oil/gas supplier ecosystem.

As local suppliers grow, I am trying to learn where follow-up breaks down: service documents, bid packs, quote status, vendor paperwork, compliance evidence, staffing or job records, customer updates, and approvals.

The useful intro is not necessarily the most senior person. It is someone close enough to the workflow to know what is still handled through inboxes, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, portals, or someone's memory.

Would there be 1-2 GOGEC or oil/gas support-service contacts worth speaking with from that operator perspective?
