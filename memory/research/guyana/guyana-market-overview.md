## The Macro Picture

Guyana is the **fastest-growing economy in the world**, driven by ExxonMobil's offshore oil discovery in the Stabroek Block (~11 billion barrels of recoverable oil).

| Year | GDP Growth |
|------|-----------|
| 2022 | +62.3% |
| 2025 | +19.3% |
| 2026 (projected) | +16.2% |
| 2027–2030 (IMF avg) | ~14%/year |

Oil revenue is flooding government coffers and the Ali administration is deploying it aggressively across infrastructure, digitization, housing, and healthcare. The government's 2026 budget was described by VP Jagdeo as arriving "early" with full funding to "keep sweet promises."

**The critical dynamic:** Massive capital inflow + under-developed institutional infrastructure = procurement inefficiency, cost overruns, and accountability gaps at scale. This is the exact environment where AI process consulting creates outsized value.

## Key Sectors & Government Initiatives

### 1. Digital Government Transformation (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
- President Ali committed to **digitizing all government services by mid-2026** (October 2025 announcement)
- Active platforms: "Citizen Portal" (government service access) and "AskGov" (AI-powered government assistant)
- **Guyana signed a 100MW AI data center MOU with Cerebras Systems** (US) in November 2025 — Wales, West Bank Demerara
- Government providing free Coursera training in AI, data management, and cybersecurity to public sector workers
- Ministry of Finance actively pursuing e-procurement modernization
- **Key gap:** Ministries told to digitize with no underlying automation infrastructure

### 2. Drainage & Waste Management (ACTIVE CRISIS — government spending now)
- Georgetown's drainage system is a well-documented crisis — most city drains are "heavily silted" with plastic waste
- President Ali announced in **December 2025**: "comprehensive plans for digital mapping of drainage and irrigation systems, starting with Georgetown"
- November 2025: "Massive technologically-driven Georgetown drainage improvement begins" (Demerara Waves)
- October 2025: Ministry of Local Government ran city-wide cleanup campaign — vendor management and waste tracking identified as broken
- January 2026: Georgetown Chronicle opinion pieces describe overflow drains, clogged systems, no real-time monitoring
- **The spend is happening now.** This is not a future plan — it's an active procurement cycle.

### 3. Infrastructure Projects (Roads, Bridges, Dams)
Active major projects:
- **Demerara Harbor Bridge** — new 4-lane high-rise, PPP model
- **Amalia Falls hydropower dam** — 165MW, ongoing financing challenges
- Extensive road and canal rehabilitation across all 10 regions
- New hospitals and healthcare facilities in every region

**Problem:** No real-time budget monitoring or project accountability layer across these projects. Contracts routinely overrun with limited visibility for ministry leadership.

### 4. Healthcare Digitization
- Ministry of Health received one of the largest budget allocations in 2025
- Electronic Health Records rollout
- Telemedicine expansion to interior regions
- Medical supply chain modernization

### 5. Agriculture & Export Logistics
- Guyana wants to be "the breadbasket of the Caribbean"
- Target: reduce imports by 25% by 2025
- Cold chain, export logistics, and agricultural processing investment ongoing

## Investment Climate — Honest Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Fastest-growing economy globally
- Culturally open to US companies and products
- Government explicitly prioritizing digitization and foreign expertise
- Active AI/tech investment (Cerebras MOU, Coursera partnerships)
- Trump administration ally — US-friendly political environment

**Risks:**
- **Procurement is political.** US State Dept 2025 Investment Climate Statement explicitly: "Widespread concerns remain about inefficiencies regarding the awarding of contracts." Personal connections matter.
- **Informal market.** "Trust and personal connections are important factors in the business environment."
- **Corruption history.** CFR (February 2026): "Guyana has long struggled with corruption, including bribery, embezzlement, and a weak rule of law."
- **Payment terms.** Government contracts in developing economies often pay 90–180 days.
- **In-person required.** Government consulting sales will require travel to Georgetown.
- **Local registration likely needed.** May need a Guyanese entity or local partner for government tenders.

**Bottom line:** The opportunity is real and large. The entry requires a local partner or warm introduction — cold RFP bids are unlikely to win without local relationships.

## Entry Strategy

**Recommended path:** Partner with a local Guyanese firm as the AI/automation specialist they bring in on their government contracts. Avoids procurement politics. Positions JT as the technology layer on existing relationships.

**Second path:** The Cerebras AI data center creates demand for AI implementation talent that doesn't exist locally in Guyana. Skills gap = consulting opportunity.

**Key question before any next step:** Does JT's contact have a direct line into a government ministry or a local firm doing government work?
