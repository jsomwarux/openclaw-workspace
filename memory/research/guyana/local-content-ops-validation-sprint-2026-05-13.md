# Guyana Local Content Ops Validation Sprint — 2026-05-13

## Go/no-go thesis

**Verdict: GO as a 10-conversation validation sprint, not as a full business line yet.**

Guyana is worth pursuing now only through warm-intro, operator-first conversations around local-content administration, procurement readiness, bid/tender tracking, compliance documents, and supplier operations. The wedge is commercially grounded enough to test because:

- Guyana has a formal Local Content Register for petroleum suppliers and contractors.
- The Ministry of Natural Resources launched a newer digital Local Content Registration Portal in 2026 to streamline supplier/contractor registration and certification.
- The Centre for Local Business Development Supplier Registration Portal is explicitly positioned around oil/gas suppliers accessing procurement opportunities.
- Local-content rules create recurring document, renewal, vendor, bid, and reporting work for suppliers.
- JT has an authentic Guyanese-American trust bridge plus a practical business-systems background, but not enough local proof to cold-pitch as a Guyana transformation consultant.

**Pursue now if warm intros exist. Watch if intros stall. Park if conversations show suppliers already have this solved or only want free advice/government access.**

The offer should stay narrow:

> A Local Content Operations Sprint for Guyana-connected suppliers: clean up compliance docs, procurement/admin workflows, bid readiness, tender tracking, vendor documents, and exception dashboards.

Do **not** lead with AI transformation, government digitization, smart-city demos, or broad country-level modernization.

## Admin/compliance pains to test

Ask operators where the work breaks. Do not assume the pain is “AI.” Test these concrete pains:

1. Local Content Register application/renewal document collection.
2. Certificate renewal dates and missing supporting documents.
3. CLBD Supplier Registration Portal profile upkeep and procurement-opportunity monitoring.
4. Tender/bid calendar management.
5. Bid pack readiness: certificates, insurance, NIS/GRA/tax documents, safety docs, capability statements, pricing sheets.
6. Vendor/customer contact records spread across WhatsApp, inboxes, spreadsheets, and people’s phones.
7. Quote follow-up and aging opportunities.
8. Invoice/payment follow-up.
9. Customs/import-export document status tracking.
10. Manpower/onboarding document packs: IDs, certifications, HSE training, medicals, contracts.
11. Safety/compliance evidence collection for customers and prime contractors.
12. Owner visibility: “what’s stuck, who owns it, what changed, what needs approval?”
13. No single dashboard for procurement, compliance, documents, and follow-ups.
14. Staff turnover leaving workflows undocumented.
15. WhatsApp/email sprawl causing missed deadlines.

## Mock workflow/demo scope

Build one lightweight demo only after 2–3 conversations confirm the same pain. Use mock data; do not imply it is live or endorsed.

**Demo name:** Demerara Supplier Ops Desk

**Mock company:** Demerara Marine Support Ltd.

**Modules:**

1. **Local Content Compliance Tracker**
   - Certificate status
   - Renewal due date
   - Required documents
   - Owner
   - Missing/expired items
   - Evidence link

2. **Tender/Bid Tracker**
   - Opportunity source
   - Deadline
   - Customer/prime contractor
   - Bid owner
   - Pack completeness
   - Next action
   - Risk flag

3. **Vendor/Customer CRM Lite**
   - Contact
   - Category
   - Last touch
   - Next follow-up
   - Active documents
   - Open quote/invoice

4. **Document Intake System**
   - Intake form
   - Folder naming convention
   - Required doc checklist
   - Missing-doc auto-reminder

5. **Exception Dashboard**
   - Renewals due within 45 days
   - Bid deadlines within 7 days
   - Missing certificates
   - Overdue quote follow-ups
   - Unassigned tasks
   - Pending approvals

**Automation ideas:**
- Daily/weekly reminders for due/overdue docs.
- Bid-deadline alert to owner.
- Intake form creates folder + task row.
- Document status changes update dashboard.
- Weekly owner summary: stuck work, deadlines, decisions needed.

**Demo outcome:** Make an operator say: “Yes, this is basically our admin mess.” If they focus on the tech stack instead of the workflow, the demo is too technical.

## 10-conversation validation plan

### Goal
Get 10 short conversations that answer whether local-content supplier/admin pain is urgent, budgeted, and reachable through trust channels.

### Conversation mix

1. Family/dad network mapping call: who knows suppliers/operators/compliance people?
2. Diaspora business contact: who is actively doing Guyana business?
3. Supplier owner/operator: logistics/marine/procurement.
4. Supplier ops/admin manager: compliance/docs/bid desk.
5. Local accountant/bookkeeper serving suppliers.
6. Local lawyer/compliance consultant serving suppliers.
7. CLBD/GCCI/AmCham/GOGEC ecosystem contact.
8. Local IT/digital firm that could partner/subcontract.
9. Staffing/manpower or safety vendor operator.
10. Procurement/logistics firm or shore-base-adjacent contact.

### Questions to ask

1. “Where does admin work pile up for suppliers trying to win or maintain oil/gas work?”
2. “What documents or renewals create the most deadline risk?”
3. “How are tender opportunities tracked today?”
4. “Who owns bid packs, certifications, vendor docs, and follow-ups?”
5. “What is still handled through WhatsApp, email, or spreadsheets?”
6. “What happens when a certificate, safety doc, quote, or bid deadline is missed?”
7. “Would a 2–3 week cleanup sprint be useful, or is the pain somewhere else?”
8. “Who would pay for this: owner, ops, compliance, procurement, or no one?”
9. “What would a useful first deliverable look like?”
10. “Who else should I talk to who sees this problem every week?”

### What to listen for

**Strong signals:**
- “We miss things because it is all in email/WhatsApp.”
- “Renewals and document packs are a headache.”
- “Tender tracking is manual.”
- “We need to look more professional for oil/gas customers.”
- “I know three companies with this problem.”
- “Can you show me what that dashboard would look like?”

**Weak signals:**
- Only wants government intros.
- Only talks about broad AI or data centers.
- Says supplier admin is handled by a dedicated ERP/compliance platform.
- No owner/budget for operations cleanup.
- Wants unpaid research or generic advice.

## Decision rule after conversations

After 10 conversations, classify as:

### Pursue now
Move into prospect list + capability brief + demo + 3 paid-pilot targets if at least 4 of 10 conversations produce strong pain signals **and** at least 2 people can introduce JT to a supplier/operator/partner with decision access.

### Watch
Keep monthly monitoring and warm-intro nurturing if pain is real but intro paths are weak, buyers are unclear, or timing is not urgent.

### Park
Stop active effort if fewer than 3 conversations confirm concrete admin/compliance/procurement pain, no one can name a buyer, or the only paths require government procurement/political access.

**Current recommendation:** Pursue the validation sprint now, but cap it at 10 conversations and one lightweight mock demo. Guyana should not consume more build/research time until warm intros and operator pain are confirmed.
