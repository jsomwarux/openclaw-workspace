## What to ask

Use these questions in calls:

1. Where does admin work pile up for suppliers trying to win or maintain oil/gas work?
2. What documents, certificates, renewals, or registrations create the most deadline risk?
3. How are tenders and bid opportunities tracked today?
4. Who owns bid packs, supplier profiles, vendor documents, customer follow-up, and compliance evidence?
5. What is still handled through WhatsApp, email, spreadsheets, or someone’s memory?
6. What happens when a document, certificate, quote, invoice, or bid deadline is missed?
7. Would a 2–3 week cleanup sprint be useful, or is the real pain somewhere else?
8. Who would care enough to pay for this: owner, ops, compliance, procurement, or no one?
9. What would make a first deliverable immediately useful?
10. Who else should I talk to who sees this every week?

## What not to ask

Avoid these because they sound too broad, political, or premature:

- “Can you introduce me to someone in government?”
- “Who controls oil/gas contracts?”
- “How can I get government work in Guyana?”
- “Do companies need AI transformation?”
- “Can I digitize this for Guyana?”
- “Who has budget for an AI consultant?”
- “Can you send this to everyone you know?”
- “Do you know Exxon contacts?” as the first ask.
- “Can you help me sell this?” before pain is validated.
- Anything implying JT has local presence, government relationships, or deep Guyana delivery history he does not yet have.

## Positioning guardrails

Use:

- “Guyanese-American”
- “family connection to Guyana”
- “workflow/admin cleanup”
- “local-content supplier operations”
- “documents, renewals, bid tracking, vendor follow-up”
- “partner-first”
- “learning whether this is actually useful”

Avoid:

- “AI transformation for Guyana”
- “government digitization consultant”
- “I can help modernize Guyana”
- “foreign consultant” framing
- “I have local access” unless a specific intro exists
- “diaspora opportunity platform” before demand exists
