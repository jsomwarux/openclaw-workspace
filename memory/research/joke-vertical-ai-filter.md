# Joke Vertical AI Filter

Created: 2026-05-15

## Purpose
Evaluate “AI for [boring vertical]” ideas without chasing every funny-sounding niche.

A vertical being unsexy is not enough. It must have repeated manual coordination, money changing hands, low software penetration, and signs that operators are already using or could quickly use AI inside routine work.

## Market Signal
OpenAI usage data shared in May 2026 suggests active entrepreneurial ChatGPT usage is mostly outside tech startups: services, retail, healthcare, trades, agencies, creators, and local operators. That makes boring verticals more credible, but only when there is a specific workflow and distribution path.

## Pass Criteria
Score each 0/1.

1. **Large fragmented market** — many independent operators, not a few enterprise buyers.
2. **Phone/DM/email scheduling or intake** — work starts in messy communication, not clean software.
3. **Manual spreadsheet/template behavior** — Google searches show templates, trackers, forms, logs, checklists, or calculators.
4. **Recurring admin pain** — scheduling, reminders, quotes, follow-ups, renewals, payments, reporting, compliance, dispatch, or customer updates.
5. **Low-tech buyer** — current workflow is texts, calls, IG DMs, email, PDFs, spreadsheets, or generic booking tools.
6. **Informal AI usage wedge** — buyer already uses ChatGPT/AI for drafts, replies, marketing, research, quotes, SOPs, or admin, but without a system around it.
7. **Clear economic event** — booking, sale, renewal, invoice, upsell, saved labor, avoided penalty, or faster collection.
8. **Narrow first workflow** — one painful workflow can be prototyped in <7 days.
9. **Distribution path exists** — directories, associations, franchise lists, local SEO, Facebook groups, trade shows, or warm intros.
10. **Data is obtainable** — sample inputs can be captured without sensitive access or heavy integrations.
11. **Human buyer value today** — valuable before agent-native commerce is mainstream.

## Interpretation
- 9–11: strong niche worth research/prospect list.
- 7–8: keep as future signal; test with one workflow example.
- ≤6: content idea only or skip.

## Default Search Pattern
Use searches like:
- `[vertical] spreadsheet template`
- `[vertical] intake form`
- `[vertical] scheduling template`
- `[vertical] invoice tracker`
- `[vertical] client follow up template`
- `[vertical] operations checklist`

## Examples to Test Later
- Pet groomers: scheduling, vaccine/intake reminders, no-show follow-up, recurring appointment prompts, IG/phone booking.
- Cleaning companies: quote intake, recurring client scheduling, supply tracking, cleaner dispatch, review requests.
- Local moving companies: quote triage, inventory forms, date availability, follow-up, claims intake.
- Med spas: consult intake, appointment reminders, package follow-up, before/after consent tracking.
- Specialty contractors: estimate follow-up, project status updates, material ETA, punch-list closure.

## Kill Rules
- Do not build just because the niche is funny.
- Do not choose a vertical if distribution is unclear.
- Do not pick workflows that require regulated advice, medical claims, or financial decisions.
- Do not make the first product a platform. Start with one workflow and one artifact.
