## What Surfaced

- Guyana oil-sector scale is accelerating: fresh local and regional coverage points to production moving past 900,000 barrels per day, with Uaru expected to push the country toward or beyond 1 million barrels per day by year-end.
- Local-content procurement is the useful operator signal, not the macro oil story. Fresh coverage cites more than 2,500 local vendors and more than USD 2.5B in procurement opportunities since oil production began in 2019.
- Reuters' recent local-content example is practical: some categories have percentage requirements for Guyanese businesses, including medical services and catering. That suggests procurement readiness, compliance documentation, vendor follow-up, and service-category qualification are likely pain points.
- Logistics and infrastructure attention is widening. Recent reporting says French companies are increasing interest in Guyana oil, logistics, and infrastructure, while Curacao is positioning Buskabaai as a regional offshore support/logistics hub for Guyana and Suriname.

## JT Move

Frame the Guyana path around **supplier-readiness operations for local-content and oil-adjacent service businesses**, not generic "AI for Guyana" or broad oil-market commentary.

Useful validation questions for connector/operator emails:

- Which supplier categories are being asked for documentation, certificates, insurance, compliance proof, or local-content registration most often?
- Where do local vendors lose time: intake, missing documents, quote follow-up, customer/vendor reminders, bid deadlines, or status reporting?
- Are operators using spreadsheets, WhatsApp, email, or portals to track procurement opportunities and follow-ups?
- Which service categories are most ready for a small workflow pilot: catering, medical, transport/logistics, construction support, facility services, equipment rental, or admin/back-office?

Best current wedge:

- "We help local-content suppliers keep procurement paperwork, follow-ups, and opportunity tracking from falling through the cracks."
- Tie the proof to existing JT work: insurance-expiration workflow, document readiness, reminder loops, and human-approved outbound follow-up.
