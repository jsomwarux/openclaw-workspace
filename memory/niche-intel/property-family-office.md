# Property / Family Office Operations

## Current Thesis
Best consulting lane right now. These buyers often do not need a new SaaS platform; they need a local-first AI operations layer around AppFolio/QuickBooks/spreadsheets/inboxes/banking reports with approvals and audit trails.

## Buyer/User Pains
- Expiring insurance/COIs/documents are easy to miss.
- Rent delinquency reports are only useful when ledgers are clean.
- Owners/CFOs need visibility into what changed, what is stuck, and what needs approval.
- Financial workflows cannot be fully autonomous; trust, approval, and audit trails matter.
- Offices are overwhelmed and rely on a few people remembering edge cases.

## Common Workflows
- Insurance expiration tracking and follow-up.
- Rent delinquency report cleanup, outreach queue, and cooldowns.
- QuickBooks/bank reconciliation and cash-timing risk checks.
- Loan/document deadline tracking.
- Vendor/tenant follow-up and owner reporting.

## Tools / Systems Used
AppFolio, QuickBooks Desktop/Enterprise, spreadsheets, local file folders, inboxes, banking portals, SMS/email, self-hosted/local PCs.

## Automation Opportunities
- Exception dashboard for expiring docs, aging receivables, cash timing, missing fields, and owner approvals.
- Data readiness checks before tenant-facing or financial automation.
- Daily/weekly owner-ready summaries.
- Audit log for every draft, approval, send, and override.

## Objections / Trust Issues
- Data accuracy is the blocker, not AI capability.
- Sensitive financial/tenant actions need human approval.
- Local-first posture matters: no SaaS lock-in, no uncontrolled data exposure.
- Buyer wants reliability and ownership, not another dashboard they must babysit.

## Content Angles
- “Never automate tenant outreach from a dirty ledger.”
- “Family offices do not need a chatbot. They need an exception layer.”
- “AI should show what is about to break before it breaks.”
- “The workflow is approval-first, not autonomy-first.”
