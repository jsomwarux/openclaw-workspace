# Construction / Field Operations

## Current Thesis
Construction buyers care less about “AI agents” and more about field notes, owner updates, job status, vendor/subcontractor coordination, and proof that work is not stuck in texts or spreadsheets.

## Buyer/User Pains
- Field notes and photos get buried in texts.
- PMs rewrite updates manually.
- Owners receive vague or late status reports.
- Punch items and blockers are inconsistently logged.
- Job status depends on who answered the phone.

## Common Workflows
- Field note/photo → punch item.
- Foreman update → PM approval → owner-ready summary.
- Change/order/status tracking.
- Subcontractor/vendor follow-up.
- Daily/weekly project digest.

## Tools / Systems Used
Texts, WhatsApp, email, spreadsheets, project-management tools, PDFs/photos, job dashboards, CRMs.

## Automation Opportunities
- Field note intake and classification.
- Owner update drafts with PM approval.
- Daily project exception dashboard.
- Photo/note OCR and trade/location/urgency extraction.
- Job completion documentation.

## Objections / Trust Issues
- Field teams will not adopt complicated tools.
- AI must not send owner updates without PM review.
- Photos/notes are messy; confidence thresholds matter.

## Content Angles
- “Construction AI should start with the field note handoff.”
- “A messy jobsite note should become an accountable workflow.”
- “Chat with project docs is less urgent than owner-ready updates.”

## Outreach Hooks
- “If field updates are stuck in texts, the PM becomes the integration layer.”
- “I can map one job-status workflow and show where automation removes handoff drag.”

## Proof Assets / JT Advantages

- Construction field note → owner update AI Ops Teardown.
- BSA/product ops background for cross-team handoffs.

## Passive-Income / App Ideas
- Field-note-to-update template.
- Contractor owner-update generator.
- Punch-item digest tool.

## Recent Signals
- Construction AI adoption continues around safety, training, and site operations; use specific workflows, not generic adoption claims.

## Kill / Defer Rules
- Avoid broad construction platforms.
- Avoid tools that require changing field behavior too much.
