# Skilled Trades

## Current Thesis
Skilled trades need simple workflow leverage: quote follow-up, scheduling, job status, customer communication, and review/referral capture. The product must be lightweight and not require operators to become software managers.

## Buyer/User Pains
- Missed callbacks and quote follow-ups.
- Scheduling chaos and job status uncertainty.
- Owner/operator is the bottleneck.
- Reviews/referrals are not systematically captured.
- Field notes do not become clean customer updates.

## Common Workflows
- Lead intake and qualification.
- Quote generation/follow-up.
- Dispatch/scheduling reminders.
- Job completion update.
- Review request and referral capture.

## Tools / Systems Used
Phone, SMS, email, Google Calendar, spreadsheets, ServiceM8/Jobber/Housecall Pro-style tools, QuickBooks.

## Automation Opportunities
- Missed-call/lead triage.
- Quote follow-up queue.
- Job completion summary and review request.
- Daily owner ops brief.

## Objections / Trust Issues
- Low tolerance for complex software.
- Need fast ROI and minimal setup.
- Automations must not annoy customers.

## Content Angles
- “The owner should not be the routing system.”
- “Most trade businesses leak money in follow-up, not workmanship.”
- “AI works when it removes one repeated handoff.”

## Outreach Hooks
- “How many quotes went cold this week because no one had time to follow up?”

## Proof Assets / JT Advantages
- AI lead qualification / CRM entry portfolio work.
- Construction/trades workflow project.

## Passive-Income / App Ideas
- Quote follow-up mini-agent.
- Missed-call triage template.
- Review-request automation kit.

## Recent Signals
- Keep skilled trades as a secondary consulting ICP unless warm signal appears.

## Kill / Defer Rules
- Avoid high-support SaaS for trades unless setup is extremely simple.

## Next Best Action
Use skilled-trades patterns as content/outreach variants after property/construction proof assets mature.
