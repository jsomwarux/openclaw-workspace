# Skincare / Glow Index

## Current Thesis
Glow Index should win by being evidence-first, skeptical, and buyer-protective. The strongest angle is not “AI skincare advice”; it is ranking products by evidence, ingredient relevance, hype risk, and safe claim language.

## Buyer/User Pains
- Skincare shoppers face hype, fake reviews, confusing ingredients, and conflicting advice.
- TikTok Shop accelerates trend cycles and bad buying decisions.
- Users want reassurance without medical diagnosis.
- Products are hard to compare across claims, price, and evidence.

## Common Workflows
- Product lookup.
- Ingredient/claim comparison.
- Dupe/category research.
- Routine-fit consideration.
- TikTok Shop trend sanity-check.

## Tools / Systems Used
TikTok/TikTok Shop, Reddit, beauty sites, INCI lists, ecommerce pages, affiliate programs, product reviews.

## Automation Opportunities
- Evidence-first product ranking pages.
- Claim-risk cards for trending products.
- TikTok Shop affiliate intelligence filtered through Glow scoring.
- Category pages and safe GEO/SEO summaries.

## Objections / Trust Issues
- No medical/dermatology claims.
- No diagnosis/treatment promises.
- No fake testimonials or fake before/after claims.
- Must cite sources/criteria and be transparent about limits.

## Content Angles
- “Most skincare rankings reward hype. This one punishes weak evidence.”
- “TikTok made me buy it is not a scoring methodology.”
- “A good skincare ranking should help you avoid wasting money.”
