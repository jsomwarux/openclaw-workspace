# Dead SaaS → Agent Company Acquisition Thesis

Date: 2026-05-04
Source: JT shared X post about buying dead SaaS companies and rewriting them as agent-native workflow companies.
Status: validation-only / future signal, not active acquisition.

## Thesis
Many dead or stalled SaaS products contain validated workflow demand: customer lists, support tickets, churn reasons, pricing sensitivity, and feature requests. Some dashboards may be better rebuilt as agent-native workflow executors.

## Why It Fits JT
- Matches JT's AI implementation + product builder identity.
- Uses workflow analysis, customer pain mapping, support-ticket mining, and agent implementation.
- Could create passive/semi-passive product upside if acquisition cost is low and distribution is validated.
- Could also become consulting/IP: “SaaS workflow autopsy → agent-native rewrite.”

## Why It Is Not Active Yet
- Current priority is consulting cash flow: Altmark delivery, Marketsmith conversion, proof/referral engine.
- Acquisition introduces legal, data, privacy, diligence, and maintenance risk.
- $5K-$30K purchases are not low-risk without confirmed access to customer/support data and a validated distribution channel.
- JT should not become an operator of random abandoned SaaS products before consulting revenue is stable.

## Required Gates Before Promotion
1. Acquisition cost ≤ $5K for first experiment unless JT explicitly approves higher.
2. Founder can legally transfer product assets, code, domain, brand, customer list, and support history.
3. Privacy/data rights allow analysis and compliant marketing use. No assumption that old customer data can be uploaded to ad platforms.
4. At least 100 historical users/customers OR strong niche evidence.
5. Support tickets/export reveal repeated workflow pain, not just feature noise.
6. Clear agent-native rewrite path that removes manual dashboard work.
7. Distribution path exists before build: old audience, SEO, X/Reddit niche, partnerships, or paid ads with small test budget.
8. Maintenance burden can stay low.
9. Kill condition defined before purchase.

## Smallest Validation Step
Do not buy first. Build a scanner/research list of 20 dead SaaS candidates from Acquire/PH/app stores. Score each for: niche, age, customer proof, silence/stagnation, founder reachability, data-transfer likelihood, workflow-agent fit, acquisition estimate, and distribution path. Only then choose 1-2 founders to contact for exploratory diligence.

## Trigger Condition
Promote when consulting monthly run-rate is stable at or near Safe ($10K/mo), or when a clearly underpriced asset appears with strong workflow evidence and acquisition cost ≤ $5K.

## Kill Condition
Kill/defer if the asset lacks transferable customer/support data, requires heavy product maintenance, has unclear privacy rights, or has no distribution path beyond “run ads.”
