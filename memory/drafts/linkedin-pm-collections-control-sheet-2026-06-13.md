The rent delinquency report is usually not clean enough to automate straight into tenant follow-up.

It needs a control sheet first.

Tenant. Unit. Balance. Last payment date. Payment-plan status. Owner rule. Contact method. Exception reason. Recommended next action. Reviewer. Approved action. Evidence link.

That is the layer I want in front of any property-management workflow that touches tenant messages, ledgers, or legal-sensitive follow-up.

The market is moving this way already. AppFolio connecting Realm-X to Claude signals that property-management work will increasingly start in a reasoning surface while execution still needs permissioning, platform rules, accounting context, and operator oversight.

Small operators need the lighter version of that same control layer.

For rent follow-up, the workflow can read the export, check the lease/accounting context, draft the clean next step, and hold sensitive rows for review.

It should stop on disputed balances, payment plans, missing contact data, unclear last-payment timing, owner-sensitive units, or anything close to legal notice language.

The queue matters because it shows what the agent saw, what it drafted, what it refused to send, who approved the next step, and where the proof lives.

That is the checklist I use before building this kind of workflow: intake, source of truth, approval owner, exception path, and proof.
