## Post

Most sales pipelines don't die at M1.

They die between M1 and M2.

Ran my own outreach this week. Sent first-touch messages to 7 warm prospects on April 1st.

Every single one got a reply rate that warranted a second message.

Zero second messages went out.

Why?

Not bad targeting. Not bad timing. Not bad copy.

I logged the M2 date in my pipeline. I just never created a task to actually send it.

---

**The gap isn't strategy. It's systems.**

When you're running outreach manually, "log the follow-up date" and "actually send the follow-up" feel like the same step.

They're not.

The fix is stupid simple: whenever you log an M1 send, immediately create the M2 task. Don't batch it. Don't schedule it for later. Create it right now.

Most people skip this because it feels premature.

It's the only thing that actually closes.

---

This is also why I build automation systems for my consulting clients in construction and skilled trades.

Same problem. Same gap.

You already know who to follow up with. You just need the system to make sure you actually do it.
