## LinkedIn Draft

AI adoption fails after the pilot because nobody designs the operating system around it.

Most teams can now get a chatbot, agent, or workflow demo running.

That is not the hard part.

The hard part is deciding:

- which requests enter the system
- who approves risky outputs
- where exceptions go
- what counts as saved time
- who owns stale workflows
- when the tool gets shut off

That is why the next AI enablement role looks less like "prompt trainer" and more like operations architect.

The useful layer is not another demo.

It is the intake map, owner map, approval route, value metric, and review cadence that make AI part of normal work.

No adoption system, no adoption.

The model can be good and the rollout can still fail.
