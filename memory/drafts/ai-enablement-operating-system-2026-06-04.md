## LinkedIn Draft

AI adoption needs an operating model before it needs another pilot.

Most companies collect AI use cases through meetings, Slack threads, and whoever has the loudest pain that week.

That creates a messy queue:

- no clear intake path
- no owner for each request
- no approval rule
- no value measure
- no path from pilot to normal work

The useful system is boring on purpose.

Every AI request gets mapped to:

- the workflow it touches
- the source of truth it reads
- the human who approves risk
- the metric that proves value
- the point where the work returns to the business

That is where adoption starts to look real.

Not because the model got smarter.

Because the company finally decided how AI work enters, moves, gets checked, and becomes normal operating rhythm.
