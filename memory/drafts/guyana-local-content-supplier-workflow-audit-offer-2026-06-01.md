## Buyer-facing one-pager

### Local Content Supplier Workflow Audit

Guyana's local-content opportunity is creating more work for suppliers, not just more opportunity.

The problem usually is not ambition. It is the operating layer around the opportunity: bid packs, supplier documents, quote follow-up, compliance evidence, approvals, customer updates, staffing/service records, and open items spread across inboxes, WhatsApp, folders, and spreadsheets.

The Local Content Supplier Workflow Audit is a short review of one workflow that is already creating admin drag. The goal is to make the stuck work visible, assign ownership, and show the simplest path to a cleaner process.

This is not a broad technology-overhaul project. It is a practical workflow audit for operators who need better visibility before admin gaps become missed deadlines, weak bid readiness, or credibility problems with larger customers.

## Best-fit workflows

- Bid or tender readiness: required documents, deadlines, pack completeness, owner, and next action.
- Supplier document collection: certificates, tax/NIS/GRA docs, insurance, safety records, capability statements, renewals, and missing evidence.
- Quote and customer follow-up: aging requests, open questions, customer updates, vendor dependencies, and approval status.
- Service or logistics coordination: staffing, service records, job documents, exceptions, customer updates, and handoffs.
- Compliance reporting: evidence collection, local-content reporting inputs, renewal dates, and who owns each item.

## What JT reviews

1. The current workflow from request or opportunity through document collection, approval, submission, and follow-up.
2. The places where work gets stuck, duplicated, buried, or owned by no one.
3. The files, spreadsheets, inboxes, WhatsApp threads, portals, and trackers currently involved.
4. The minimum tracker, checklist, reminder, or automation that would reduce the admin burden.
5. The handoff rules the team would need so the process keeps working after the audit.

## What the buyer gets

- A current-state workflow map.
- A bottleneck list with owner, impact, and fix priority.
- A document/checklist structure for the chosen workflow.
- A simple exception view: overdue items, missing docs, aging requests, unassigned follow-ups, and upcoming deadlines.
- A recommended lightweight implementation path: tracker only, tracker plus reminders, or tracker plus automation.
