A delinquency export, a maintenance request, a vendor note, and an owner email should not become four separate follow-up systems.

The workflow has to decide what each item is, who owns it, what can be drafted, what must wait, and where the proof goes.

That is the Exception Control Plane I want to package from the property-management proof.

For rent delinquency, the source input is the balance report. The classification is clean follow-up, payment plan, disputed balance, missing contact, owner-sensitive unit, or legal-sensitive hold. The owner queue names the reviewer. The approval rule says what can be sent and what stays blocked. The audit log keeps the source row, draft, approval, action, and evidence link together.

That same pattern is useful outside property management.

Construction project risk has RFIs, change orders, schedule slips, and missing documents.

Wholesale has messy orders, AR exceptions, inventory conflicts, and customer-specific pricing.

Insurance has underwriting packets, claims intake, renewal status, and confidence thresholds.

The AI reading the request is only the intake step.

The operating layer is the queue, approval rule, owner, and measurable event after the request is read.

That is what I want the proof asset to show: one source input, one classification path, one review queue, one approved action, and one record of what happened.
