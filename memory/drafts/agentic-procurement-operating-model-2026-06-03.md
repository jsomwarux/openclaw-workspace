## LinkedIn Draft

Your procurement workflow has to survive bad inputs before it deserves an agent.

A purchase request comes in as an email, spreadsheet row, portal note, or Slack message.

It may be missing the vendor record, cost center, SKU, policy approval, delivery date, contract terms, or budget owner.

That is where the agentic operating model starts.

The workflow reads the request, normalizes the fields, checks policy, finds the missing approval, drafts the clarification, and routes the edge case to the right owner.

The requester keeps asking for what they need in the normal way.

Finance gets cleaner spend records before bad data reaches the ERP.

That is the part most teams need before autonomous purchasing:

- intent map
- policy map
- human review rule
- source-of-truth check
- value metric
- named owner

Build that first.

Then automate.
