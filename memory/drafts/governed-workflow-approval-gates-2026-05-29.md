## LinkedIn Draft

The fastest way to make an AI workflow feel risky is to let it act before anyone knows what it saw.

For property operations, the useful version starts smaller:

Classify the report.
Separate clean rows from exceptions.
Route legal, disputed, recent-payment, missing-contact, and cleanup records away from tenant-facing drafts.
Show the reviewer exactly what will happen next.

That is the difference between a demo and an operating process.

The workflow should answer five questions before it touches a customer:

Who owns the output?
What systems can it read?
Which actions are approval-only?
Where do exceptions go?
What proof log will the owner review?

AI in operations is less about sounding smart and more about staying inside the business rules.

The machine handles the queue. The owner keeps the judgment.
