## Why this matters now

Most SMB “AI” projects start with a chatbot. That is usually the wrong starting point.

Your problem is not that customers, tenants, vendors, or prospects need more chat. The problem is that requests arrive through too many channels, with missing details, unclear urgency, and no clean handoff to the person who owns the next step.

The AI Intake + Exception Desk fixes that first.

It does not replace your team. It protects their time.

## Best-fit buyers

- Property managers handling leasing, maintenance, vendor, and tenant requests
- Construction or trade operators fielding estimate requests, job updates, and urgent service questions
- Wholesale/service teams dealing with repetitive order, quote, ETA, or document requests

## Common symptoms

- Leasing or estimate inquiries arrive after hours and go cold
- Maintenance/service requests are missing photos, address, urgency, or access details
- Staff repeatedly answer the same vendor/customer questions
- Managers cannot see request volume, response times, or repeated bottlenecks
- Urgent issues live in the same inbox as routine questions
- AI feels useful in theory, but nobody trusts it enough to let it touch real workflows

## What gets built

A controlled intake and escalation layer for one high-friction workflow.

Typical components:

1. **Structured request capture**  
   Collects the fields your team actually needs before a human gets involved.

2. **Approved-answer knowledge base**  
   Answers only from source-of-truth policies, FAQs, forms, and instructions.

3. **Exception routing**  
   Escalates emergencies, legal/financial issues, high-value opportunities, and anything outside policy.

4. **Ops queue integration**  
   Sends clean summaries to the right place: email, Google Sheets, Airtable, CRM, Slack, Telegram, or your existing tool.

5. **Weekly manager report**  
   Shows request volume, qualified leads, escalations, repeated questions, incomplete requests, response-time patterns, and estimated time saved.

## Safety boundaries

This is designed for operational trust, not AI theater.

The system will not:
- Give legal, financial, medical, fair-housing, or lease interpretation advice
- Promise pricing, availability, approval, or timelines unless explicitly approved
- Pretend to be human
- Take irreversible actions without human approval
- Hide unresolved requests from managers

The system will:
- Disclose it is automated when appropriate
- Escalate uncertain or sensitive requests
- Keep an audit trail of what came in and where it went
- Route only the minimum useful information needed for the next step
