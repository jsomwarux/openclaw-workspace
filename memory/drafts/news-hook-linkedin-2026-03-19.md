## Post

82% of insurers believe AI will define the industry's future.

14% have fully integrated it into their operations.

That gap isn't hesitation. It's infrastructure.

The AutoRek report that surfaced this also found that the average insurer manages 17 separate data sources feeding their premium processes. Two-thirds handle more than 10. Manual reconciliation on top of that is — their word — "mathematically unsustainable."

I built an Agentforce agent that handles insurance claim intake: routes by type, classifies urgency, creates the Salesforce record — before a person touches it. The hardest part of building it wasn't the AI logic.

It was mapping which system holds what data, and getting those systems to talk.

That's the actual implementation problem. Not "does AI work." It clearly does.

The firms that close the gap in the next 18 months won't be the ones that believed in AI first.

They'll be the ones that got their data architecture right before their competitors did. That window is still open — and it's closing faster than the belief numbers suggest.
