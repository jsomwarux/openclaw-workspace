The gap between "we have AI tools available" and "our teams are actually using them well" is not a product problem. It is a translation and partnership problem, and most organizations do not have anyone dedicated to closing it. That is the role you are building.

I spent 6 years at Spectrum Enterprise as the connective layer between technical teams and the business departments that had to adopt and use what those teams built. I facilitated discovery sessions with department leaders, designed adoption programs for non-technical users, and served as the visible, accessible point of contact for teams navigating unfamiliar tools at scale. In independent consulting work since, I have applied the same approach to AI directly: opening every client engagement with a use case discovery conversation, helping teams define the problem they are actually trying to solve before touching a tool, then building practical AI-supported workflows and guiding adoption through hands-on working sessions. A construction client in NYC commissioned two follow-on projects immediately because the team actually used what we built together.

At Axios, I would bring that same partnership-first approach to every department. Office hours, advisory sessions, and targeted workshops would be structured around real team workflows, not abstract AI concepts. I would draw on direct, hands-on experience operating AI workflows across research, content, outreach, and operations to show teams where AI genuinely helps, translate responsible use principles into lightweight guidance they can apply immediately, and capture repeatable patterns across departments so teams learn from each other's progress rather than starting from scratch.

Happy to walk through how I have structured discovery sessions or adoption programs on a call, or share examples of the playbooks from the consulting work.
