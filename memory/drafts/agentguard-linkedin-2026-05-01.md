## Draft

Most AI demos skip the part where businesses actually get hurt:

The agent takes an action.
Nobody knows why.
Nobody knows what data it used.
Nobody knows who approved it.
And when it breaks, the “demo” has no audit trail.

That’s the gap I’m building around with AgentGuard.

Not another chatbot wrapper.

A governance layer for AI workflows:
- what the agent was allowed to do
- what data it touched
- what action it recommended
- when a human had to approve
- what got logged for review later

The more companies add AI into ops, the less the hard part is “can the model answer?”

The hard part is:

Can the business trust the handoff?

Especially in workflows like vendor dispatch, sales ops, claims intake, recruiting, customer support, or finance exceptions.

The winning AI systems won’t just be smarter.

They’ll be observable, constrained, and easy to audit.

That’s the lane I’m interested in.
