A property manager can export 40 delinquent balances and still not know which rows an AI workflow is allowed to touch.

That is the part I would fix first.

Not the message draft.
Not the chatbot.
Not the collection language.

The first useful layer is the resolution ledger:

- source row
- balance and payment context
- missing info
- risk flag
- proposed next step
- reviewer
- approve, edit, hold, or escalate state
- evidence link
- final outcome

Salesforce just pushed pay-per-resolution pricing for support agents.

The interesting part is the standard behind it: a resolution only counts when the issue actually closes. Escalations, negative feedback, and unresolved cases do not get treated like completed work.

Property management needs the same discipline before AI touches resident, owner, ledger, or legal-sensitive follow-up.

A delinquency workflow should not get credit because it drafted a message.

It should get credit when the approved next step is logged, the exception leaves the queue, and the evidence is attached to the source record.

That is the difference between automation that looks productive and a workflow a property team can actually trust.

The record matters before the agent.
