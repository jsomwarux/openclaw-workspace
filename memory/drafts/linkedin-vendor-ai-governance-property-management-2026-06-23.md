# Vendor AI Governance For Property Management - LinkedIn Draft - 2026-06-23

## Draft

Property managers are going to inherit more AI from their software vendors before they get more time to govern it.

That is the implementation gap I am planning around in the property-management proof work.

The vendor may ship the assistant, the summary, the recommended reply, or the next-best action.

The operator still owns the resident impact, the owner relationship, the ledger risk, and the final record.

The readiness question I care about is:

It is whether the team can answer:

- which source record did this use
- what property, unit, resident, vendor, balance, date, or lease detail shaped the recommendation
- what context was missing
- what action was allowed
- who reviewed the sensitive cases
- where the approved decision landed afterward

That checklist is why I keep turning property-management AI work into control records before trying to scale the automation.

A vendor feature can generate a good-looking draft and still leave the operator exposed when the path from source data to approved action is invisible.

The stronger system is quieter:

clean records move,

sensitive records pause,

and the manager can reconstruct exactly what happened without digging through inboxes, portals, and spreadsheets.

That is the part I want to prove before I call the workflow ready.
