A studio owner with bridal alterations, sewing classes, and custom commissions has three different follow-up paths hiding on one website.

That kind of business needs every inquiry to land as an operating record.

The record should capture:
- request type
- event or class date
- garment or service details
- photos or measurement notes
- policy question
- urgency
- next owner
- follow-up state

Clean inquiries can get a drafted reply.

Sensitive ones go to review:
- wedding timeline
- refund policy
- unclear alteration scope
- custom quote
- class capacity
- missing photos

The website keeps doing what it does.

The business gets a queue that shows what came in, what is ready, what needs approval, and where the next dollar is stuck.

I would sell that operating layer to local service businesses before I sold them anything that sounds like a chatbot.

Public interest -> owner-reviewed action -> follow-up proof.
