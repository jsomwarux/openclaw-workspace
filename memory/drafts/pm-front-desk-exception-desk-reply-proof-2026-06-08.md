## Short Reply

The front desk captures the request.

The manager still needs the path after intake:

- Which record wins?
- Who approves the sensitive case?
- What gets drafted but not sent?
- What turns into a review item?
- Where does the final status get logged?

For property management, I would build the exception desk before expanding the resident/vendor flow.

## DM Version

Saw your note on property management follow-up.

The workflow I would pressure-test first is a front desk plus an exception desk.

The front desk captures the incoming work: missed call, tenant email, maintenance request, rent follow-up, vendor update, owner note.

The exception desk holds anything that should not be trusted yet: missing source data, owner approval, expensive vendor action, legal-adjacent wording, duplicate records, or a sensitive tenant issue.

That gives the manager a review queue instead of another inbox.

I put together a checklist for this: intake source, system of record, approval owner, exception path, and review queue. Happy to send it if useful.
