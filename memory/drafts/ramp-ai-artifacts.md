**Artifact 1: Aya Construction Dashboard**

A NYC construction management firm was tracking 32 buildings and 313 rooms across 8 disconnected data sources — Google Sheets, WhatsApp threads, manual status calls. Each team member was spending 3+ hours a week just figuring out where things stood. I built a React dashboard that pulled all 8 sources into a single real-time platform via the Google Sheets API, giving the team live visibility across every building and room with zero manual aggregation.

The client saw the value immediately. Within 60 days of delivery they commissioned a second engagement ($2,500) and referred a new client. That's the signal that matters — not the tech, but whether the people using it come back for more.

**Artifact 2: OpenClaw AI Agent Infrastructure**

Running a solo consulting practice means dozens of repetitive workflows: prospect research, content publishing, job market monitoring, crypto intelligence, outreach sequencing, automated backups. Doing any of it manually doesn't scale. So I built the infrastructure to not do it manually — 39 cron-driven autonomous agents running 24/7, each with a defined scope, cost controls, error handling, and monitoring. Research pipelines fire on schedule. Content gets generated and queued. Market signals get surfaced automatically. Nothing waits for me to trigger it.

This is the same architecture I deploy for clients: AI systems that teams actually depend on, instrumented so you can tell if they're working, built to iterate without rebuilding from scratch. The difference between a demo and infrastructure is whether it runs when you're not watching. Mine does.

**Artifact 3: StreetEasy Real Estate Scraper**

A property management client had their team manually checking StreetEasy for new listings every week — 3+ hours per person, coordinating qualification decisions over WhatsApp. It was fragmented, slow, and entirely dependent on someone remembering to do it. I built an n8n automation pipeline that scrapes StreetEasy on a schedule, filters listings by their qualification criteria, and delivers a structured report automatically.

The manual research time went to zero. More importantly, the WhatsApp coordination loop got replaced by a clean, repeatable workflow — listings come in pre-qualified, decisions happen faster, and no one's chasing down data. That's what workflow automation actually looks like when it works: the team's operating pattern changes, not just the tool they use.
