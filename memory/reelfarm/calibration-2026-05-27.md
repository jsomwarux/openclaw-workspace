# ReelFarm Intel Calibration - 2026-05-27

Source: JT review of May 15-23 ReelFarm Intel recommendations.

## Confidence Labels

Until a TikTok account has at least 20 posts with normal, non-throttled distribution, cap every ReelFarm recommendation at:

`Medium - hypothesis`

Use "test", "hypothesis", or "candidate angle." Do not use "High confidence" while accounts are cold, paused, throttled, or limited to a few hundred views. A few hundred views on new/throttled accounts is TikTok noise, not performance proof.

Reserve "High confidence" only for recommendations grounded in real post-performance data from normal distribution.

## Automation Slotting

Automation A = lifestyle-photo hooks.

Use A for:
- conflict
- identity
- opinion
- external-authority disputes
- feature reveals
- hooks where the text can stand alone over a lifestyle photo

Automation B = screenshot-demo hooks only.

Use B only for the pattern:

`Here is the product working, explained in 5 slides`

Slotting test before assigning Automation B:

`Does this hook promise the viewer will see the product working in the following slides?`

If no, it is Automation A.

## Brand Casing

Render app names in proper case:
- Vista
- Nash Satoshi
- Glow Index

Do not use all-caps brand names in hook recommendations. Hooks also do not need the app name in the hook slide when the conflict carries the hook; the body slides and caption can deliver the brand.

## Banned Hook Families For Slideshows

Avoid trend-locked constructions that need original audio/video context, e.g. "someone yelled [hashtag] but [app] did X." These read as confusing static text.

Avoid interactive-format hooks that imply the viewer can interact with the post, e.g. "pick the X and [app] shows Y." Slideshows cannot deliver that interaction.

Allowed exception: hooks describing what the app does, e.g. "Rate five movies and Vista names your taste."

## Strong Signal To Keep

Cross-source convergence matters more than any single hook.

When several newsletters or trend sources independently point to the same app angle, surface it as its own finding. Current examples:
- Vista: rating-precision conflict
- Nash Satoshi: contradiction / anti-hype

## Hook Tone

Favor declarative hooks over question prompts. Statements should deliver a payload; questions make the viewer do work.

If the same idea can be phrased as a question or a statement, default to the statement.

## Future Shift

After account warming resumes and enough posts get normal distribution, shift analysis from external trend adaptation to actual JT post-performance data. Confidence can rise only when real distribution data supports it.
