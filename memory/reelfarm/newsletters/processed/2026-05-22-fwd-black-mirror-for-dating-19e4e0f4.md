It’s like Black Mirror for dating.

A new website has been mysteriously spreading across TikTok by making one
promise that feels way too futuristic to scroll past:

Describe your ideal partner, and it will find the closest real dating
profile across Tinder, Hinge, Bumble, Hoppy, and more.

The website is called *Order Your Dream Match
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/V4m0Oczo5qkFQo8cZd%2BFPlyt4gJK87vzq%2B%2B9n8LStt1mhauo60iM%2F47FS8IF%0AIfbXLs1aGlKe%2BgEWWqNnYS1C%2F28jfhwWofAIIwOL%2Ft5JG6m4nL2m0YKpY7XI%0AlQFcJy1sr%2F%2F22Fnk8we9LSGSQJkkqj%2Fsgm1%2B6Dvs8yGV%2BbAew7yvtY4jebAX%0AfdbOKQQrQ9eg0p4FFVDStnscy9Z0XHoplA%3D%3D%0A/f5b87a000c16429d>*,
and the first impression feels closer to a social experiment than a dating
app campaign.

Users can select the traits they want in a partner, from appearance to
personality, and the site turns the whole thing into a custom dating search
engine.

It is easy to see why people started calling it “Black Mirror for dating.”
The idea feels uncomfortable in the exact way that makes people want to try
it.
The viral discovery

Order Your Dream Match first appeared around April 17, and for a few weeks
it looked like another strange internet experiment. Then, on May 14, one
video changed the entire campaign.

A dating app ambassador, @sushiiturtleprincess, posted a shocked reaction
video with the hook:

“girls I fear technology has gone TOO far bc why can i literally order a bf
online and it will find matching dating app profiles for me”

The video hit *3M views*, with *56K saves* and *96K shares*.

The format is easy to follow. She reacts to the idea, opens the website on
her laptop, chooses the type of person she wants, filters traits like
hairstyle, facial hair, and personality, then reaches the final result: a
real dating profile. The screen says that out of *318 matches*, this person
was *#1*.

That ending is what turns the video from curiosity into conversion. Viewers
get to watch someone create a fantasy partner, then see an actual profile
appear at the end. The curiosity peaks right when the site asks them to
continue into the app.
The plot twist

The clever part is that Order Your Dream Match appears to search across
many dating apps. The website mentions Tinder, Hinge, Bumble, Hoppy, and
more, which makes the experience feel like a broader dating search engine.

Then people start seeing the same weird pattern.

The final result seems to lead to one dating app again and again.

*Hoppy.*

That is the real campaign.

Hoppy

is a dating app that did around *60K downloads* and *$6K MRR* last month.
Before this wave, the app was already running UGC through ambassadors,

Why it converts

The funnel works because the download arrives at the most emotional point.
Users start on a free browser-based website, describe the person they want,
watch the site return a matching profile, and reach the app store when they
already feel invested in the result.

Hoppy becomes the next step in the experience, rather than another dating
app asking for attention.

Hoppy turned a dating app promo into a discovery game. The landing page
creates the fantasy, the demo creates the proof, and the final profile
creates the desire.

People come for the dream match, and Hoppy gets the install.
