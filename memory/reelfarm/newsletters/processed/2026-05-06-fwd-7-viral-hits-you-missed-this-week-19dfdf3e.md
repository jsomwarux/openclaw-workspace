in the family breaks tragic news in a comedic and unexpected way.

All videos start with a UGC reaction and end with an explicit CTA.

insane 9.9 million views

SideMe AI is an app that teaches you how to code, calling itself the
“Duolingo for coding.” Not only do they follow that strategy strictly, they
even copied the same mascots in different colors and place them side by
side in widgets and app features in every single post.

This week, one carousel hit 1.4 million views
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/kBPaD9cbSerKzYDdbNOlfbIR11mE3kk1bjq3KUHexINAH6Q3o0tFhBOOGCdO%0AdJ9PtBDK7fYxG7QQwOSOtN8q9WW62kfTjtpP30wMjU36tofMNzmm81hWp8CY%0ABHPafu4n8Js7yX2eH3kXjeORruHPxDQxGS793bP8YAcIy5Biz%2Bj3Itl7mC0u%0AMbf4PJyRrYL9816ofY7%2FVr6MBuWgstuSeQ%3D%3D%0A/2e9cba70b95ed9da>

of the app, showing a Duolingo-like menu with coding terms.

No Contact is a simple app that tracks how many days you go without
contacting your ex.

Four days ago, one of their carousels reached 586k views
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/cu8%2BrklDh3FUsXQdrUBSAdiPDFbQ3Kw3mXH846xSr2vYbRJM%2FB4g4wLYoMpJ%0Aq5HVuwO8DQIxizwwdp%2BMg0ESF6cGhF8IggrfVOHWv%2FLu16YeiSn%2B9od2aynD%0AkTefu4nHeXfVm2OMuVLpO7f2Bx2wnfoMx6%2BWC%2BojEsj0bH40pElVrqaI%2FesV%0ASDsHHwn6jK31lDrCzVqGTBrcPj837%2FILeg%3D%3D%0A/7ef675490ea17b9f>

A few slides later, he shows that he gets a text from his ex asking to
meet. The final slide then displays his streak through a screenshot of the
app.

saved money.”
