We tracked what’s been going viral across short-form this week, and one
format we hadn’t seen before.

Five formats kept showing up across language learning, AI chat,
fact-checking, creator tools, and fashion apps. Different categories, but
all the same underlying mechanics.

Here’s your quick feed of what is pulling the heavy views lately.
*The Chaotic AI Tutor Format*

Pingo AI
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/%2FneNa7moSj15h4ktF6rCbT568WO1rcul%2Bt6WkDyBEHg9B0NTUFJuGPrxtwNc%0ALmUUvtqKnas%2FkX%2BJbdFNEyC8YZuJTMhvo1lPIe7qdnqYiPn2JhZ6Lhxve6UZ%0AgfkxO%2B0CD8%2FIxh2tntzNag0hXtI0Jj49GvY5dhcusdJWJ%2F9NyfyArwF3pU3r%0AGOvroSoTPIWbO%2Bv4TdDGY2JUXAVnlAQN4g%3D%3D%0A/3691c84d5f7b3790>
has been pushing videos where creators film themselves extremely close to
the camera, crying or panicking because a language feels impossible.

The AI tutor responds with something blunt, chaotic, or slightly insulting,
and that loop is the whole video.

The frustration is real, the AI response is unpredictable, and the whole
thing can be made again tomorrow with a different language.
*The Language Test Format*

LangAI
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/cPpjJ5bjUkBH%2FbEFbEAtOl%2Bd4mU%2Fc%2F7hMeBZkDVEEVvHaP1%2Bg7E6lD1vmxud%0AHZ%2B6v0vo%2FEX2zOiit9gJvOZCL6GaoZNoQWL%2F3Ih75rnJVRT7j5sYySeDqDOp%0AO%2Fu7gBE%2B5attYA8URgheVpq%2Bx2q%2F8v7zslP58zL6tbVCCAxPbKtG7nRV9sjQ%0A7nlSnqgRPxJlLtduxPsjTM5Bp3%2F8bR%2ByOQ%3D%3D%0A/e0a506e5b7a053aa>
found traction with animated characters and comprehension challenges.

Viewers get a challenge, learn something without realizing it, and then
spend the next five minutes arguing in the comments about whether they
passed.
*The Shocked Close-Up + App Check Format*

AI fact-checking app Verifii
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/QUL5wyaVZ267i4LiS6aMmw%2BEjWJFJXDyDpGH4S4KpYVQoXYzncEsHQ4jBTOa%0AlgB%2Fa0ClN4jeYeuQwktSaOmA%2FPAHM%2BfKaXn6ll%2FB5xepvm%2FhUGnSfONOdj20%0A9M%2BZVjHnZlyxRJtFVfY4CK9gZPJacRspGXLU1g6N%2BSw%2FsMKSYe6yAtKS2bUU%0AFtVic2vkuo5%2BE3bpueDfcyGhLiCGlEuY%2FQ%3D%3D%0A/ce9d7b94541482a3>
is using news stories to capture attention.

The creator shows a strange claim, runs it through the app live, and the
whole video lives in the two seconds before the answer appears.
*The Two-Slide Notes Format*

ReelRise
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/7fr%2BsMF%2FExCMzk9xLTF%2B0gxkgQ4OavuZOpWdWxgisExxDNmHjRLXyhVu4veK%0AfHEu5T1z6apHx7%2FS%2FiH0uTjfPHLc2A%2FyCPe2C%2Fj3yMTRiLk1mpjd4G1DEui6%0AzCr9LVEfhibSJam2ZUAqkmglYl7SvxAnzr7cS3BkK5HxuLdCehqHq8Mqjcbz%0AUiv8o7i7XBxqTH%2Bzb2tqm0wk1vRFrbHQtQ%3D%3D%0A/8d14ee9dcaf117b7>
cracked a Pinterest slideshow structure.

A face with a hook on the first slide, then an iPhone notes-style
screenshot with advice and the app name on the second.

It feels close to the “show your iPhone notes” trend, but repackaged as
creator-growth advice.
*The Competitive Attack Format*

Genies
<https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/v1/c/f288fxuw32EL%2BIm%2Btie0ZxentgOuX7jsfqhQ%2BeGpaLWLXV2fpeVDLALDwEQO%0ALqatkVwTzOtfTl%2FqsQHkhPopq2T1Dlt53M5rwSjQRA1fcB0Jo4rO9QtwcfnE%0AprLKny1j%2F6ZND8gEB8L5mV4D6xWHNIPg%2BPVjV%2FFCGuJpmMLjwzgqIiNBnVWU%0AIK0c9SUjNuhHBE80n%2Bm9Hj5rqZsn6iLgAw%3D%3D%0A/3149f427324a5f8b>
broke through with a creator shooting close headshots on a red-washed
background, with hooks that went straight at competing apps and suggested
the alternative was getting worse.

The red-screen aesthetic helps, but the real driver is giving viewers
permission to feel like they’ve been using the wrong thing.
*The One to Watch*

Out of all five, the Shocked Close-Up + App Check feels the most genuinely
new, and it’s coming from a niche that barely exists yet.

The whole strategy is a shocked selfie, a viral claim, and an AI verdict on
camera.

Perfect for UGC scaling, and easy enough to run with AI-generated clips
without ever needing a real person on camera.

If one-tap fact-checking becomes a habit, the category has a lot of room to
grow, and right now almost nobody is in it.
