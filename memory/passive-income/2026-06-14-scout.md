## 6 Raw Ideas

### RECIPECLEAN
**Concept:** A recipe organizer that turns saved TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and messy blog recipes into clean cook cards, pantry tags, grocery lists, and repeatable meal boards for home cooks who save more recipes than they ever cook.
**Lateral connection:** Social recipe chaos + personal knowledge-base cleanup.
**levelsio equivalent:** Small utility with a narrow transformation job, closer to PhotoAI's "upload input, receive useful output" loop than a broad meal-planning app.
**JT stack fit:** OpenClaw/n8n can ingest links, extract text, normalize ingredients, and generate cards. Next.js can ship the frontend quickly.
**Revenue model:** $9/mo for unlimited recipe cleanup plus $5 one-shot "clean my 50 saved recipes" import.
**Longevity signal:** People will keep saving recipes across unstable platforms; the value is control and repeatability, not a trend-specific meal app.
**Research signal:** Google Trends showed `recipe organized` at +1816.7% over 30 days; food signal file also shows related kitchen utility interest.
**Behavioral demand:** Control and embarrassment avoidance: users hate knowing they saved hundreds of recipes but cannot find the one they want at dinner time.
**TrustMRR pattern:** Adjacent to productivity/content tooling; no direct comp in TrustMRR, but transformation utilities with low support burden match the reported creator/productivity pattern.
**Agent-native angle:** A household or meal-planning agent could call it to turn saved links into structured grocery JSON and recipe cards.
**Creativity check:** Not a standard top-10 passive-income idea; adjacent to meal planning, but narrower and more behaviorally concrete.

**JT stack fit:** Similar to Glow Index, but for pet food and claims. Existing ranking-app pattern, content templates, and affiliate surfaces apply.
**Revenue model:** Affiliate revenue plus $7/mo premium shelf scans and saved pet profiles.
**Longevity signal:** Pet health spend is recurring, emotionally charged, and product choice is confusing.
**Research signal:** Exploding Topics showed dog bone broth, dehydrated dog food, urinary-care cat food, wet pet food, automatic dog feeder, and Litter-Robot as high-growth lifestyle/pet signals.
**Behavioral demand:** Reassurance and fear of waste: owners want to know whether expensive pet products are actually appropriate before buying.
**TrustMRR pattern:** Adjacent to analytics/data/attribution plus consumer ranking pages; no direct pet comp in visible records.
**Agent-native angle:** A shopping agent could request a structured `safe_for_pet_profile` output before buying food or accessories.
**Creativity check:** Pet affiliate sites exist, but a claim-risk and profile-matching layer is more specific than generic pet product reviews.

**TrustMRR pattern:** Adjacent to creator/social-commerce tooling and consumer analytics; monetization is affiliate plus premium utility.

**Creativity check:** Makeup content is crowded, but lookup by product/shade/photo is more specific than generic beauty advice.
