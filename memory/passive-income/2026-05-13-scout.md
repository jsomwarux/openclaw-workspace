## 6 Raw Ideas

### 1. WalkthroughProof [M9-Vision] [M11-AgentDesk]
**Concept:** A tenant-side move-in/move-out photo inspection app that turns room photos into a timestamped defect ledger, checklist, and dispute-ready PDF/ZIP. Audience: renters, small landlords, and property managers who need proof without learning full property inspection software.
**Lateral connection:** Rental inspection anxiety + AI vision/OCR + property/family-office audit-trail workflow.
**levelsio equivalent:** PhotoAI-style one-purpose visual transformation, but the output is an evidence artifact rather than a pretty image.
**JT stack fit:** Next.js upload flow + low-cost vision model to detect scratches/stains/missing fixtures + Claude to write neutral descriptions + PDF generator + optional webhook/API. JT's property niche intel gives approval/audit language and avoids over-automation.
**Revenue model:** $9 one-off inspection report, $19/month renter plan for unlimited reports, $49/month small-landlord plan with branded reports and storage.
**Longevity signal:** Security deposit disputes, move-in condition reports, and tenant-landlord documentation are secular and recurring; photos are already part of the workflow.
**Research signal:** Brave found recent Reddit demand around definitive rental inspection checklists and photos: r/shitrentals asked for a “Rental Inspection Checklist” 3 weeks ago; other Reddit threads advise taking photos of everything. Competition exists for property managers (`Property360`, `Keyturn`, PHOTO iD), but the renter-first dispute-proof angle is thinner and simpler.
**Behavioral demand:** Control/reassurance + fear of loss. A renter acts before moving because $9 feels cheap compared with losing a $1,500 deposit or being blamed for old damage.
**Agent-native angle:** A relocation/renter agent could call this during a move checklist, return JSON defects + PDF evidence, and store the artifact in a tenant vault. Price: $9/report. Output: PDF + JSON room/defect schema + source photo hashes.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Not a Shop product; short-form distribution is “scan your apartment before move-in” demos with before/after proof screenshots.
**Creativity check:** Would not appear in generic passive-income lists; it is a narrow evidence-artifact product.

### 2. PickyBowl Index [M6-ExplodingTopics] [M8-API] [M12-SocialCommerce]
**Concept:** A dog food topper ranking engine for picky dogs, sensitive stomachs, budget-conscious owners, and kibble-specific pairings. It scores toppers by ingredient quality, calories, price-per-serving, storage convenience, allergy flags, and owner use case.
**Lateral connection:** Exploding “dog food topper” search growth + Open Food Facts/brand data + affiliate/TikTok Shop comparison pages.
**levelsio equivalent:** Glow Index/Nash Satoshi pattern: automated rankings in a product category where buyers currently rely on messy reviews.
**JT stack fit:** Reuse Glow-style ensemble ranking, crawl ecommerce/brand pages, ingest Open Food Facts where available, use Claude for claim-risk summaries and safe non-medical copy. Existing App Marketing OS can generate category pages and TikTok scripts.
**Revenue model:** Affiliate fees from Chewy/Amazon/TikTok Shop + $5 personalized “topper match” report for dog age, kibble, sensitivities, and budget.
**Longevity signal:** Pet owners spend emotionally and repeatedly; picky eating, aging dogs, and digestive concerns do not disappear after the trend cycle.
**Research signal:** Exploding Topics shows `Dog food topper` at 24x growth, 27,100 monthly searches, CPC $1.04. Brave found comparison content from Business Insider, Whole Dog Journal, BetterPet, Chewy, and We Feed Raw, which validates commercial demand but leaves room for a transparent scoring/ranking layer rather than editorial listicles.
**Behavioral demand:** Care identity + reassurance. Owners act when a dog refuses food, ages, or has stomach issues; “am I helping or wasting money?” creates urgency.
**Agent-native angle:** A pet-care shopping agent could request a ranked shortlist with constraints (budget, allergies, kibble type), receive JSON products + affiliate links + reason codes. Price: free via affiliate or $2 API lookup.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Affiliate-first. Content format: “3 toppers for picky senior dogs ranked by price-per-serving and ingredient simplicity.” Compliance risk: avoid medical claims; position as product comparison, not veterinary advice.
**Creativity check:** “Pet affiliate site” is generic; “dog food topper scoring by use case and kibble pairing” is narrow enough to survive.

## levelsio Portfolio Gap Observations
- The strongest gaps are not broad “AI apps”; they are narrow confidence artifacts: inspection proof, quote proof, product proof, route proof, applicant proof. Each converts messy input into a decision-ready output.
- PhotoAI/InteriorAI-style visual transformation still has room when the result is a practical artifact, not aesthetics. WalkthroughProof is stronger than generic “AI home inspection” because the buyer wants evidence, timestamps, and dispute leverage.
- RemoteOK/Nomads-style directories still work when the underlying category has messy, recurring comparisons. Dog toppers and route constraints have enough ongoing search/social signal; perfume finder is too crowded this week because incumbents already rank.
- “Alternative to Notion/Airtable/QuickBooks” searches are saturated at the horizontal layer. Better wedge: sit above old vertical software as an exception dashboard with no migration.
- Agent-native monetization should be an output-format upgrade, not the main story. Human buyer value comes first; JSON/API/x402 comes later.
