### 1. EarStack Studio [M6-ExplodingTopics] [M9-Vision] [M12-SocialCommerce]
**Concept:** Upload a photo of your ear and get a stacked-lobe / curated-ear plan: suggested piercing placements, healing schedule, jewelry sizes/styles, and affiliate/POD shopping links. Audience: people planning multiple lobe/cartilage piercings who currently rely on Reddit opinions and piercer consultations.
**Lateral connection:** Exploding Topics' `stacked lobes` growth + AI vision/photo layout + jewelry affiliate commerce.
**levelsio equivalent:** PhotoAI for one hyper-specific visual transformation, with a directory/affiliate layer for jewelry and piercers.
**JT stack fit:** Next.js upload flow, low-cost vision model for ear landmarks and spacing, Claude for plan generation and safety disclaimers, affiliate feeds for jewelry, pSEO pages for “stacked lobe ideas for [ear shape/style].”
**Revenue model:** $7 one-time curated-ear plan; $12/mo style board for people building a multi-month ear stack; affiliate commission on jewelry and aftercare.
**Longevity signal:** Piercing/body-jewelry identity is durable, visual, and repeat-purchase heavy. Stacked lobes are a trend, but “what should I do with my ear next?” is a recurring beauty/body-mod job.
**Research signal:** Exploding Topics: `Stacked lobes` 70x growth, 12,100 monthly searches. Web search found multiple recent Reddit posts about planning, opinions, and showing stacked lobes: `r/piercing: How do you plan out and schedule your piercings?`, `Stacked lobes opinions`, `Got stacked lobes done today`.
**Behavioral demand:** Status/taste identity + embarrassment avoidance. Users want their ear to look intentional, not randomly punctured; they act before booking because placement mistakes are semi-permanent and expensive to fix.
**TrustMRR pattern:** Adjacent to AI content/creator-commerce and CPG/commerce enablement patterns (Brand On Demand/Supliful, Vid.AI, Speel.co) but not a copycat: the paid artifact is a personal visual plan plus affiliate shopping, not generic content generation.
**Agent-native angle:** Weak-to-moderate. A personal-shopping agent could buy a $5 JSON “ear stack plan” while assembling gift/jewelry recommendations for a human, but first buyer is human. Output: JSON plan + rendered image + affiliate product list.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Affiliate, not inventory. Short-form formats: “AI planned my ear stack,” before/after mockups, “3 placements that make stacked lobes look messy.” Support risk low if clearly non-medical and “confirm with licensed piercer.”
**Creativity check:** Not a top-10 passive income idea. It is niche, visual, and commerce-native.

### 2. SubSwap Chef [M7-GTrends] [M8-API] [M13-Behavioral]
**Concept:** A recipe substitution engine for people who are mid-cook and missing one ingredient, but optimized for outcome risk: “will this ruin texture/flavor/moisture?” rather than generic substitutions. Audience: home cooks, meal-preppers, allergy households, and small food creators testing recipes.
**Lateral connection:** Google Trends breakout for `ingredient substitution` + Spoonacular/USDA/Open Food Facts data + AI risk scoring.
**levelsio equivalent:** A tiny paid AI tool for one specific transformation: unavailable ingredient → ranked safe swaps with measurement conversion and outcome warnings.
**JT stack fit:** Spoonacular + USDA FoodData Central for ingredient/nutrition data, Claude for reasoning, Next.js tool page, pSEO pages for thousands of “[ingredient] substitute in [dish]” queries, optional camera OCR of pantry labels later.
**Revenue model:** Free 3 swaps/day; $5/mo for unlimited swaps, allergy profiles, saveable pantry, and printable recipe-adjustment cards; affiliate links for specialty ingredients.
**Longevity signal:** People will keep cooking with missing ingredients, dietary constraints, and budget substitutions. The need is not trend-dependent.
**Research signal:** Google Trends: `ingredient substitution` +107.1% 30-day breakout. Web search found existing generic tools (LogicBalls, Galaxy), which validates demand but leaves room for a specialized “risk of ruining the recipe” UX instead of a prompt wrapper.
**Behavioral demand:** Control/certainty + fear of waste. The trigger is immediate: someone is already cooking and does not want to waste the whole meal or make another store run.
**TrustMRR pattern:** Adjacent to AI utility/subscription and API-validation patterns, not directly copied from any TrustMRR comp. The subscription is small but tied to repeated household workflow, similar to narrow tools that charge for recurring outputs.
**Agent-native angle:** Moderate. A meal-planning or grocery agent could call an API endpoint to convert recipes around pantry constraints and return structured substitutions with confidence scores. Price: $0.05/call or $10/mo API key. Output: JSON with substitution, ratio, risk, allergens, source ingredients.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Mostly affiliate/digital. Content: “I tested viral substitutions,” “best swaps for egg-free brownies,” “don’t use this substitute in cookies.” Low return risk because no inventory.
**Creativity check:** Generic recipe apps are listicle bait; this narrower “mid-cook substitution risk engine” clears the bar.

**Lateral connection:** Property-management spreadsheet pain + agent approval queues + JT's local-first client proof patterns.
**levelsio equivalent:** More “micro-SaaS desk” than classic levelsio; closest to a niche operations dashboard with a weekly summary artifact.
**JT stack fit:** JT already has property/family-office workflow context, local-first n8n/OpenClaw patterns, owner-summary thinking, and approval-boundary guardrails. Build can start as CSV/email intake + dashboard + draft follow-ups, not a full PM system.
**Revenue model:** $49/mo for up to 25 active work orders/vendor threads; $149/mo for family-office/local-first install template; optional $199 setup checklist sold as digital product.
**Longevity signal:** Vendor coordination is secular: even when PM software exists, contractors still reply late, updates live in texts/emails, and owners need clean summaries.
**Research signal:** Web search: Atlas Global Advisors cited 64% of PM firms still relying on spreadsheets for core operations; a 2026 Reddit result says spreadsheets broke past 10 doors, multiple apps were open, and “vendor coordination getting updates from contractors is always the bottleneck.” Niche intel confirms property/family-office buyers need exception dashboards, approvals, and audit trails more than chatbots.
**Behavioral demand:** Control/reassurance + embarrassment avoidance. The buyer acts when an owner asks “what’s happening with this repair?” and the answer is buried across texts, email, and someone's memory.
**TrustMRR pattern:** Local business/agency software and analytics/data comps show reported MRR in the $39K-$178K range. This is adjacent to local ops software, not a DM Champ/lead-gen clone: it monetizes operational certainty and owner-ready summaries.
**Agent-native angle:** Strong. A property ops agent working for an owner/manager could call the desk mid-week to fetch `stuck_items.json`, `next_actions.csv`, and a source-cited owner summary. Human buyer value exists today; agents benefit later from clean schemas, provenance, and approval states.
**TikTok Shop angle:** None. This is B2B subscription/template revenue.
**Creativity check:** Not generic property management software. It is a narrow vendor-follow-up exception layer.
