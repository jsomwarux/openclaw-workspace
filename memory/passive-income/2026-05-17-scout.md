## 6 Raw Ideas

### 1. LabelLaunder [M6-ExplodingTopics] [M9-Vision] [M12-SocialCommerce]
**Concept:** A photo-first garment-care decoder for people buying expensive cashmere, wool, silk, and delicate resale clothing. User snaps the care label or product page, gets a plain-English wash/dry-risk score, detergent recommendation, shrink/bleed warning, and “safe at home vs dry-clean” guidance.
**Lateral connection:** Exploding cashmere-shampoo interest + resale/luxury clothing anxiety + vision/OCR label interpretation.
**levelsio equivalent:** PhotoAI-style narrow visual transformation, but the output is a risk report/recommendation instead of an image.
**JT stack fit:** Vision OCR + Claude interpretation + affiliate links to detergents/mesh bags/steamers + simple Next.js lookup pages. No ML engineering; reusable pSEO pages for “how to wash [fabric/brand]” queries.
**Revenue model:** Free label checks with affiliate commerce; $7 one-off “closet care audit” PDF; $6/mo premium for saved garments, reminders, and travel/laundry mode.
**Longevity signal:** Delicate/luxury clothing, resale, and care-label confusion are secular. People will keep buying expensive fabrics and fearing one bad wash.
**Research signal:** Exploding Topics: `Cashmere Shampoo` 99x+ growth, 1,600 monthly volume, CPC $0.32. Web validation found Reddit users asking which cashmere shampoos to use and sources explaining that iPhone label-symbol lookup is useful but not a full fabric/product risk workflow.
**Behavioral demand:** Fear of loss/waste + embarrassment avoidance. The trigger is immediate: “I might ruin a $250 sweater tonight.”
**Agent-native angle:** Low priority but viable: wardrobe/resale agents could call `/care-risk` before listing or recommending garments; output JSON/PDF with fabric, care symbols, risk score, products.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Affiliate/POD-light. Content format: “I scanned this $180 cashmere sweater label and here’s why most people ruin it.” Low return risk if recommending low-claim laundry products, but avoid unsupported fabric guarantees.
**Creativity check:** Not a generic passive-income idea; specific enough to survive.

### 2. SafeRun Routes [M7-GTrends] [M8-API] [M9-Vision]
**Concept:** A route-quality planner for women, night runners, heat-sensitive runners, and travel runners that scores routes by lighting, shade, traffic exposure, water/bathrooms, elevation, and “would I actually feel comfortable here?” rather than distance alone.
**Lateral connection:** Running route planner breakout + safety apps + Open-Meteo/weather + map/street-image/POI analysis.
**levelsio equivalent:** Nomads.com-style ranked directory meets a one-shot AI planning tool.
**JT stack fit:** Open-Meteo for weather/heat, map APIs/OSM for roads/parks/lighting proxies, Strava/GPX import where available, Claude for route explanation. JT can ship a web planner before a full mobile app.
**Revenue model:** $5 route pack for travelers, $9/mo for saved safe routes/weather-aware reroutes, affiliate links to running lights/vests/gear.
**Longevity signal:** Running is durable, travel running is recurring, and safety/heat/shade concerns do not disappear with better watches.
**Research signal:** Google Trends: `running route planner` +783.3%. Web validation found women-runner safety app coverage, Legs on Tour helping women find safe routes, and Reddit users sharing route-tracking/safety tools. Generic route planners exist, but safety/shade/context scoring is thin.
**Behavioral demand:** Control/reassurance. The user acts before a run in an unfamiliar place, at dusk, or during hot weather — not someday.
**Agent-native angle:** Travel agents could buy a $3 safe-run route PDF/JSON for a hotel itinerary; output includes route, risk flags, weather window, and citations.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Affiliate only. Short-form format: “I scored 3 popular NYC running routes for shade + safety after 7PM.”
**Creativity check:** Route planners are common; safety/shade/travel-run scoring is narrow enough to avoid listicle territory.

### 3. ScaleSub Chef [M7-GTrends] [M8-API]
**Concept:** A substitution calculator for home cooks and macro trackers that converts “I’m missing X” into gram-accurate swaps that preserve texture, calories/macros, allergens, and cooking role — especially for baking, meal prep, and high-protein recipes.
**Lateral connection:** Ingredient-substitution breakout + smart kitchen scale trend + nutrition APIs.
**levelsio equivalent:** A single-use AI utility like InteriorAI: one specific transformation, paid per report for serious users.
**JT stack fit:** USDA FoodData Central/Open Food Facts/Spoonacular from the API library + Claude rules + optional photo/OCR of recipe ingredient list. Can start as a web tool with no hardware integration.
**Revenue model:** Free simple swaps; $6/mo for macro-preserving substitutions, saved recipes, batch meal-prep conversions; affiliate links to smart scales and specialty ingredients.
**Longevity signal:** People will always cook with missing ingredients, dietary constraints, and macro goals. The need is not trend-dependent.
**Research signal:** Google Trends: `ingredient substitution` +839.3%. Web validation surfaced multiple substitution charts/tools plus an AI ingredient substitution review framework; existing tools skew generic and do not deeply handle grams/macros/texture constraints.
**Behavioral demand:** Convenience + fear of wasting a recipe. The trigger is immediate: the pan is out, the recipe is open, and one ingredient is missing.
**Agent-native angle:** Meal-planning agents could call the API when pantry constraints conflict with a recipe; output JSON with substitution ratios, macro delta, confidence, and warning notes.
**TikTok Shop angle:** Affiliate/low-risk. Content format: “I replaced butter in 3 brownie recipes and scored texture + macros.” Avoid medical diet claims.
**Creativity check:** “Recipe app” is generic; “role-aware gram substitution engine for macro cooks” is not.

## levelsio Portfolio Gap Observations
- The strongest open pattern this week is not “another tracker”; it is a narrow scorer layered on top of crowded behavior: running routes, home gyms, substitutions, garment care, landlord repairs, server entitlements.
- Photo-first risk scoring is still underbuilt outside obvious categories. People already take photos of rooms, garments, labels, gyms, and tenant damage; the missing product is a ranked warning/report they can trust.
- Agent-native only works when the endpoint produces a useful artifact a human already values. SafeRun route PDFs, Landlord tickets, and Tebex entitlement diffs have real human buyers now; x402/agent purchasing can be added later, not used as the reason to build.
- Glow-adjacent beauty is high-signal but increasingly crowded. This week’s best beauty-like idea is actually garment care/fragile-fabric buyer protection, not another PDRN/perfume tool, because PDRN Decoder and ScentTwin were already scouted.
