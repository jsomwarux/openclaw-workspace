## Problem Section

Most property and family-office operations do not fail because the team lacks software.

They fail in the gaps between systems:

- insurance dates tracked in spreadsheets
- COIs and vendor documents sitting in folders or inboxes
- rent delinquency reports that need manual review before outreach
- owner updates rebuilt from stale exports
- QuickBooks, AppFolio, banking, and spreadsheet data that do not tell one clean story
- approval-sensitive work that cannot be handed to AI without controls

The risk is not that nobody works hard.

The risk is that important work ages silently until someone asks:

- What is expired?
- What is missing?
- Who owns this?
- What changed since last week?
- What needs approval?
- Why did nobody see this earlier?

That is where AI operations should start.

Not with a chatbot. Not with a broad transformation plan. With the exception layer your team can actually use.

## Example Workflows

## Insurance Expiration Exception Layer

For teams tracking insurance, COIs, vendor documents, or policy dates manually.

What it does:

- scans the source spreadsheet/report/folder
- flags 30/14/7-day upcoming expirations
- flags overdue, missing, malformed, or duplicate records
- drafts follow-up from approved templates
- routes follow-up for human approval
- logs every status change

Best first build when the team wants a low-risk proof point with immediate operational visibility.

## Rent Delinquency Readiness Queue

For teams that want collections visibility but do not trust the data enough for automation.

What it does:

- ingests a delinquency report
- validates required fields
- separates clean rows from review-needed rows
- flags stale, duplicate, payment-plan, missing-contact, or do-not-contact cases
- drafts outreach only for approved clean rows
- requires human approval before anything is sent
- logs source report date, amount, draft, reviewer, approval, and status

Best first build when the right answer is not “send faster.” It is “make risky outreach safer.”

## Owner / Vendor Exception Dashboard

For teams that rebuild owner updates manually and lose visibility into blocked work.

What it does:

- pulls from approved reports/files
- flags aged, blocked, missing, changed, and approval-needed items
- groups by property, owner, vendor, workflow, severity, and age
- produces a weekly operator/owner summary
- keeps status changes and notes in an audit trail

Best expansion build when the buyer wants one review path for “what needs attention this week?”
