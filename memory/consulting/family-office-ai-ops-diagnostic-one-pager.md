# Family Office / Property Ops AI Operations Diagnostic — One-Pager

Created: 2026-05-12

## Who This Is For
NYC family offices, real estate operators, and property/finance teams that run important back-office workflows through a mix of local files, QuickBooks/Desktop-style systems, AppFolio exports, spreadsheets, inboxes, banking portals, and manual follow-up.

## Positioning
You probably do not need another generic SaaS tool. You need the systems and reports you already rely on to produce a cleaner exception layer: what changed, what is overdue, who owns it, what needs approval, and what should never happen automatically.

## Diagnostic Promise
In a short diagnostic, JT maps the highest-risk operational workflows, scores them for automation readiness, and recommends the first system worth building.

The output is not an AI brainstorm. It is a ranked implementation plan with data readiness, approval boundaries, dependencies, and a practical first-build scope.

## Best-Fit Workflows
- Insurance / COI / vendor-document expiration tracking
- Rent delinquency reporting and approved outreach prep
- Loan and document deadline tracking
- QuickBooks / bank / cash-risk review queues
- Owner-ready weekly exception briefs
- Local-first workflow hosting where sensitive data should stay inside the business

## What The Diagnostic Covers
1. Current-state workflow map
2. Source systems and report/export readiness
3. Bottlenecks, manual copy/paste points, and owner handoffs
4. Exception inventory: overdue, missing, risky, ambiguous, escalation-needed
5. Human-in-the-loop rules: what can be drafted, flagged, summarized, or prepared — and what must require approval
6. Ranked use cases by ROI, risk, data readiness, implementation complexity, and proof/referral value
7. Recommended first build with acceptance criteria, timeline, and dependencies

## First-Build Scoring
A workflow is a good first build when it is:
- Frequent enough to matter weekly or daily
- Owner-visible or tied to revenue, risk, deadlines, compliance, cash, or tenant/vendor communication
- Based on a report/export/source file the team can produce reliably
- Rules-heavy enough for automation but sensitive enough to keep human approval
- Narrow enough to show value in 1–2 weeks

## What JT Usually Builds First
A local-first exception dashboard or workflow host that:
- Watches a specific source report/file/folder
- Flags missing, overdue, changed, or approval-needed items
- Produces a daily/weekly summary
- Keeps logs/audit trail
- Prepares actions or messages for human approval
- Leaves sensitive records inside the client environment where appropriate

## Example First System
Insurance expiration tracking for a property/operations team:
- Source report/spreadsheet reviewed on a schedule
- Upcoming expirations and overdue items flagged
- Daily summary produced for the operator
- Logs/audit trail available
- No autonomous financial or external action
- Acceptance measured by records tracked, exceptions surfaced, review time saved, and missed-expiration risk reduced
