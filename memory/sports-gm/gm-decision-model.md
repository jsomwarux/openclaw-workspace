# GM Decision Model

## Market Inefficiency Definition
A dynasty inefficiency is a measurable gap between market price and probable future value.

Market price = current crowd valuation.
Probable future value = expected value over the relevant dynasty window, based on production, usage, role security, age curve, team context, liquidity, and catalysts.

## Scores
1. Price Score
2. Production Signal
3. Usage Quality
4. Role Security
5. Age / Window Fit
6. Liquidity
7. Catalyst

## Recommendation Labels
- Buy
- Hold
- Sell
- Avoid
- Contender Buy
- Rebuild Buy
- Liquidity Flip
- Watchlist

## Confidence Tiers
Low = one meaningful signal.
Medium = 2-3 independent signals.
High = market price disagrees with history + usage + multiple market sources, with a clear catalyst and bounded risk.

## Publishing Rule
Every take must include the risk case. No fake certainty.
