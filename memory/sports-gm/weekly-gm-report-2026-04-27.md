# Weekly Dynasty GM Report — 2026-04-27

## Market Thesis of the Week
Post-draft dynasty markets are most exploitable when managers confuse **new information** with **complete information**.

Draft capital and landing spot matter, but the crowd usually overreacts in two directions:
1. rookies with clean narratives get priced like solved assets before they earn NFL usage;
2. veterans who absorbed depth-chart competition get discounted before role loss is proven.

The GM edge is not being early on every player. It is separating durable signal from emotional repricing.

## Roster Construction Principle
Do not build a dynasty roster around player rankings. Build it around **asset liquidity by team window**.

A rebuilding roster should prefer assets that can gain market value without needing weekly points.
A contender should prefer assets that convert roster value into lineup advantage.
A trapped-middle team should stop collecting “good players” and make a directional bet.

## Draft Capital / Trade Market Note
The most common dynasty leak is treating all young assets as liquid. They are not.

A future 1st is liquid because every manager can project their own hope onto it.
A rookie becomes less liquid the moment he has NFL usage, depth-chart friction, or market disappointment attached.

That means draft season is often a liquidity conversion trap: managers turn flexible capital into fragile narratives.
