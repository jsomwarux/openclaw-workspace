# Daily Reset Protocol — JT's Nervous System Pattern

## The Pattern
Centrally-driven protective tension: chronic subconscious muscle bracing maintained by three interlocking mechanisms:
1. **Autonomic dysregulation** — sympathetic dominance keeping muscles "on"
2. **Structural compensation** — gunstock deformity → kinetic chain asymmetry (pelvis/ribcage/jaw)
3. **Respiratory restriction** — inability to fully exhale, locking the system in extension

**Downstream symptom**: voice dysfunction (tension in vocal mechanism)

## Daily Protocol (~7 minutes total)

Run in this order. Each step feeds into the next.

---

### Step 1 — Autonomic Downregulation (2 min)
*Target: sympathetic dominance*

**Physiological Sigh** (do 2x first):
- Double inhale through nose (short sniff, then full inhale)
- Long, slow exhale through mouth (6-8 seconds)
- Fastest known nervous system downregulator. Do this before starting the rest.

**Extended Exhale Breathing** (10 cycles):
- Inhale 4 counts
- Exhale 8 counts (twice as long as inhale)
- The exhale length is the mechanism — this is what activates the parasympathetic brake
- Do NOT force the inhale. Let the exhale do the work.

---

### Step 2 — Jaw Release (1 min)
*Target: structural asymmetry + sympathetic bracing site*

- Teeth apart (not clenched, not exaggerated open — just separated)
- Tongue resting on the floor of your mouth (not pressing the roof)
- Soften the masseter muscles (the ones that clench)
- Hold 30 seconds while continuing to exhale-extend breathe
- Then: slow jaw circles — 5 clockwise, 5 counterclockwise
- Then: gently open as wide as comfortable, hold 3 seconds, release slowly

The jaw and the pelvic floor share nervous system tone. Releasing the jaw releases the whole system.

---

### Step 3 — Ribcage + Thoracic Decompression (2 min)
*Target: respiratory restriction + structural extension lock*

**Seated or lying:**
- Place both hands on your lower ribs (sides)
- Inhale and let your ribs expand OUTWARD into your hands (360-degree expansion, not just chest lift)
- Exhale fully — feel the ribs compress inward and DOWN
- The goal: ribs moving out AND down with breathing, not just chest lifting
- 8 slow cycles focusing on feeling the lower rib movement

**Ribcage lateral stretch:**
- Right arm overhead, lean left slowly — feel the right side of your ribcage open
- Breathe INTO the stretch — ribs expanding against the restriction
- Hold 20s each side

---

### Step 4 — Kinetic Chain Reset (2 min)
*Target: structural compensation from gunstock deformity*

**Pelvic neutral reset:**
- Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat
- Find neutral pelvis: not arched, not flattened — mid-position where lower back has a small natural curve
- Breathe here for 5 cycles, letting your body settle

**Shoulder asymmetry release:**
- Still lying: let both arms fall to your sides, palms up
- Notice which shoulder sits higher/more forward — consciously let it drop
- Slow shoulder circles on the higher side only: 10 circles backward
- Then: gently reach both arms overhead, hold 20s, return slowly

**Hip/psoas release:**
- Single knee-to-chest: pull right knee to chest, hold 20s
- Feel the difference between sides — the side with more restriction = where the kinetic chain loads
- Switch sides

---

### Step 5 — Voice Warm-Up (optional, 1 min)
*Target: downstream voice dysfunction*

- Lip trills (motorboat sound) up and down your pitch range — 30 seconds
- "Mmm" hum with jaw loose — feel the vibration in your face, not your throat
- Siren slide from low to high — easy, not forced
- This releases tension in the laryngeal mechanism that the bracing pattern locks

---

## When to Run This

- **Minimum**: once daily, any time (morning preferred — sets the nervous system tone for the day)
- **Ideal**: twice daily — morning + before bed
- **Emergency reset**: if you feel maximum bracing, just do Steps 1 + 2 (3 minutes)

## Tracking Markers
Check these to gauge progress:

1. **Exhale test**: sit quietly and exhale completely. Can you exhale more after you think you're done? (Yes = still restricted)
2. **Jaw gap**: teeth apart, measure with fingers — are you getting more space between upper and lower teeth over time?
3. **Shoulder symmetry**: stand in front of mirror, relax — does one shoulder sit higher? Track which side and by how much
4. **Voice ease**: does speaking feel free or tight? Any pressure in throat or chest when talking?
5. **Activation baseline**: first thing in the morning, before checking phone — what's your baseline bracing level (1-10)?

## Key Insight
The pattern maintains itself because the three mechanisms are interlocked. Stretching alone (structural only) will keep reverting because the nervous system keeps signaling tension. Breathing alone won't fix asymmetry. The protocol hits all three simultaneously — this is why order matters.
