# Personal Training Session Plan — 2026-07-02

Purpose: two PT sessions for JT. Session 1 targets chronic compensation/bracing issues. Session 2 targets patellar tendinopathy / jumper's knee return-to-basketball prep.

## Known Pattern
- Gunstock deformity compensation with kinetic-chain asymmetry.
- Chronic sympathetic bracing with jaw/neck/trap/shoulder/hip involvement.
- Respiratory restriction: difficulty fully exhaling, extension-lock tendency.
- Recurrent jaw/Eustachian/voice tension tied to upper cervical and breathing mechanics.
- Jumper's knee concern before returning to basketball.

## Session 1 — Compensation / Bracing / Injury Prevention

Ask the trainer to teach and check form on:

1. 90/90 hip switches with active internal/external rotation
   - Goal: hip rotation without lumbar twisting or jaw bracing.

2. Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch with posterior pelvic tilt and exhale
   - Goal: open hip flexor/psoas without arching the low back.

3. Single-leg Romanian deadlift
   - Goal: posterior-chain loading, pelvis level, foot tripod, no neck/jaw tension.

4. Step-down from low box
   - Goal: knee tracks over second/third toe, pelvis level, slow eccentric control.

5. Split squat or rear-foot-elevated split squat regression
   - Goal: unilateral leg strength without dumping into the front knee.

6. Dead bug with full exhale
   - Goal: ribs down, pelvis controlled, breathing maintained under core load.

7. Face pull with external rotation
   - Goal: scapular control, no shrugging, no neck takeover.

8. Landmine press
   - Goal: upper-body pressing that respects carrying-angle asymmetry and avoids jaw clenching.

9. Loaded carry, preferably suitcase carry
   - Goal: anti-lateral-flexion core control, ribcage stacked over pelvis.

Trainer should assess:
- Single-leg squat/step-down symmetry.
- Hip internal rotation difference side-to-side.
- Ankle dorsiflexion.
- Shoulder/scapular asymmetry.
- Whether jaw/neck bracing appears under load.

## Session 2 — Jumper's Knee / Basketball Return

Ask the trainer to teach a progressive tendon-loading sequence:

1. Spanish squat isometric
   - 4-5 x 30-45 seconds.
   - Pain should stay tolerable, ideally <= 3/10.
   - Use for pain modulation and tendon loading entry point.

2. Leg extension isometric, mid-range
   - 4-5 x 30-45 seconds at roughly 30-60 degrees knee flexion.
   - Single-leg if tolerated.

3. Heavy slow resistance squat pattern
   - Goblet squat, hack squat, leg press, or safety-bar squat depending on form.
   - Slow tempo: roughly 3 seconds down / 3 seconds up.
   - Goal: progressive quad/tendon capacity.

4. Decline squat or heel-elevated squat regression
   - Start double-leg, progress toward single-leg only if symptoms allow.
   - Use slow tempo, not bouncing.

5. Step-down progression
   - Low box first; increase height/load only when next-day pain is stable.

6. Split squat / rear-foot-elevated split squat
   - Use as the main single-leg strength bridge.

7. Calf raise and soleus raise
   - Basketball needs ankle/calf capacity, not only quads.

8. Landing mechanics
   - Snap-downs, drop landings, and stick landings before repeated jumps.
   - Quiet landing, knee tracks over toes, hips absorb force.

9. Low pogo hops
   - Only after strength work is tolerated.
   - Begin with small amplitude and low volume.

Progression rules:
- During exercise: pain <= 3/10 and stable.
- Next morning: pain should not be worse than baseline.
- No pickup basketball until single-leg step-downs, split squats, and low-level hops are tolerated without next-day flare.
- Return to basketball should progress: shooting only -> light drills -> controlled 1v1/2v2 -> limited pickup -> full pickup.

Avoid in early return:
- Max vertical jumps.
- Repeated hard cuts.
- Full-court pickup.
- High-volume plyometrics before tendon capacity is rebuilt.

Sources checked:
- AAFP summary of APTA patellofemoral pain guideline: posterior hip + quadriceps strengthening, step-downs/squats/lunges/hip band work.
- Breda et al. BJSM/PubMed: progressive tendon-loading exercise therapy outperformed eccentric-only therapy at 24 weeks for patellar tendinopathy.
- Patellar tendinopathy clinical management literature: isometrics, heavy slow resistance, and progressive quadriceps loading are core rehab tools.
